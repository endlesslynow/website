The Sherefname

Description

The sources examine Şerefxanê Bedlîsî's Şerefname, a significant 16th-century historical work written in Persian about Kurdish dynasties, tribes, and rulers. They provide context on the author's life, shaped by his experiences between the Safavid and Ottoman empires, highlighting the volatile political landscape of Kurdistan during this rivalry. The sources explain the structure and content of the Şerefname, detailing its focus on various Kurdish ruling houses and its importance as a foundational text for Kurdish historiography. They also discuss the work's complex legacy and interpretation, particularly its role in modern Kurdish national narratives and the ongoing academic debates surrounding its "ethno-political" significance. Finally, the sources touch upon the history of Şerefname translations and the need for a comprehensive critical edition.
