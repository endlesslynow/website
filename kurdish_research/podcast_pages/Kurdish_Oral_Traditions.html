Kurdish Oral Traditions

Download Audio File

Description

This report explores Kurdish oral traditions before 2010, highlighting their crucial role in preserving cultural identity, history, and language despite political suppression and displacement. It focuses on the dengbêj, revered singer-storytellers who transmitted epics, history, and social norms through performance. The text examines diverse genres including epic romances, shorter songs, folktales, proverbs, and riddles, detailing their content and performance contexts. It also discusses the challenges faced by these traditions, such as linguicide and modernization, alongside revitalization efforts like the establishment of Dengbêj Houses.
