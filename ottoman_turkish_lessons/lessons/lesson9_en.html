1. Basic Adjective Use

The Turkish adjective, whether used as a predicate or as an attribute, remains unchanged.

Examples:

Ottoman TurkishTransliterationTranslation
او كوچوك درév küchük dürthe house is little
بيوك بر آدمböyük bir adéma great man
المالر طاتلی درélmalar tatli dirthe apples are sweet
بيوك آدملرböyük adémlerthe great men

2. The Derivative Adjective اسم منسوب

The derivative adjective, called اسم منسوب is'mi ménsoub, is made by adding various suffixes to nouns.

3. Suffix I: ـلی، ـلو -li, -lu (Possession)

This suffix indicates possession of the thing designated by the noun.

Examples:

Ottoman TurkishTransliterationTranslation
صو ⟵ صولوsou ⟵ soulouwater ⟵ watery, fluid
یر ⟵ یرلیyér ⟵ yérliplace ⟵ fixed in a place; native
او ⟵ اولیév ⟵ évlihouse ⟵ that has a house; married
آت ⟵ آتلی، آتلوat ⟵ atli, atlouhorse ⟵ horseman
عزت ⟵ عزتلوiz'zét ⟵ iz'zétlouhonour ⟵ honorable

4. Suffix ـلی، ـلو -li, -lu (Nationality)

With the proper names of men or places, the same suffix indicates a native or an inhabitant of those places or connexion of those persons.

Examples:

Ottoman TurkishTransliterationTranslation
آمریقا ⟵ آمریقالیAmériqa ⟵ AmériqaliAmerica ⟵ American
عثمان ⟵ عثمانلیOsman ⟵ OsmanliOsman ⟵ Ottoman
تركیا ⟵ تركیالیTürkiya ⟵ TürkiyaliTurkey ⟵ an inhab. of Turkey, Turk
مرذیفون ⟵ مرذیفونلیMérzifoun ⟵ MérzifounlouMerzifon ⟵ a native of Merzifon

10. Suffix IV: ـجی -ji (Trades)

This suffix, added to a noun, indicates the individual who exercises a trade or calling connected with the first noun.

Examples:

Ottoman TurkishTransliterationTranslation

توفنکجیtüfénkjigun maker
صوجیsoujouwater seller
بکمزجیbékmézjitreacle seller

13. Suffix VI: ـسز -siz, -suz (Privative)

This is a privative adjectival suffix, meaning without, void of, lacking, free from, -less.

Examples:

Ottoman TurkishTransliterationTranslation

یولسزyolsouzroadless; impolite
اتسزétsizfleshless, thin
یوزسزyüz'süzwho has no face; shameless
صاغسزsaghsizunhealthy, weakly

Derivative Nouns

Derivative nouns are made by the addition of the following particles to the nouns.

14. Derivative Nouns: Suffix ـلق، ـلك -liq, -lik (Place)

Joined to nouns it expresses a place peculiar to the thing named, or a place where it abounds.

Examples:

Ottoman TurkishTransliterationTranslation
پابوجلقpaboujlouqplace where the slippers or boots are left
آغاجلقaghachliqa place where the trees abound
کومورلكkéomürlüka place where coal is deposited
طاشلقtashliqa place where stone abounds, stony; stone-pit

English:

place where the slippers or boots are left
a place where the trees abound
a place where coal is deposited
a place where stone abounds, stony; stone-pit

15. Derivative Nouns: Suffix ـلك، ـلق -lik, -liq (Abstract)

This suffix, added to an adjective, forms its abstract noun.

Examples:

Ottoman TurkishTransliterationTranslation

ایيلكéyilikkindness
چوجقلقchojouqlouqchildishness
فقيرلكfaqîrlikpoverty

16. Derivative Nouns: Suffix ـلك، ـلق -lik, -liq (Trades)

18. Derivative Nouns: Suffix ـجق، ـجكز، ـجك -jiq, -jik (Diminutive)

باباجق، باباجغزbabajiq, babajighazpapa
آنهجق، آنهجكزanajiq, anajighazmama
قزجغزqizjighazpoor little girl
