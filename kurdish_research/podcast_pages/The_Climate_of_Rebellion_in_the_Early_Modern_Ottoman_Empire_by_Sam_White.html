Description

This document is an excerpt from "The Climate of Rebellion in the Early Modern Ottoman Empire" by Sam White, published in 2011 by Cambridge University Press. The text explores how environmental factors, particularly climate change during the Little Ice Age, and related demographic pressures contributed to social unrest and rebellions in the Ottoman Empire, notably the Celali Rebellions of the late 16th and early 17th centuries. It examines the Ottoman imperial ecology, focusing on resource management, provisioning systems, and land use policies, alongside population growth and migration patterns. The source also discusses the historiography of the Ottoman decline, offering a reinterpretation of the 17th century as a period of turbulence and transformation rather than simple decay, and makes a significant contribution to the emerging field of Near Eastern environmental history by integrating climatic explanations with social, economic, and political interpretations of events.
