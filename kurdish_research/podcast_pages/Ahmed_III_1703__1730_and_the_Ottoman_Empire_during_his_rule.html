Ahmed III 1703 1730 and the Ottoman Empire during his rule

Description

The provided source is an extensive historical report that offers a detailed analysis of the reign of Ottoman Sultan Ahmed III (1703–1730), framing it as a crucial period of crisis, adaptation, and profound contradiction. The text meticulously chronicles the era's major events, including the military victory against Russia at the Pruth River and the disastrous defeats by the Habsburgs leading to the Treaty of Passarowitz. A large focus is placed on the Tulip Period (Lale Devri), an unprecedented cultural renaissance characterized by artistic patronage, the establishment of the first Turkish printing press, and a growing engagement with Western European culture, which was led by Ahmed III and his Grand Vizier, Nevşehirli Damat İbrahim Pasha. Ultimately, the source explains that this era of elite opulence created deep social resentment and economic strain, culminating in the Patrona Halil Rebellion of 1730, which violently ended the Tulip Period, forced the Sultan's abdication, and severely hampered future reform efforts in the empire.
