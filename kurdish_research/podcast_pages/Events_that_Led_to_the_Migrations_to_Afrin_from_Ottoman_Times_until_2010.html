Events that Led to the Migrations to Afrin from Ottoman Times until 2010

Download Audio File

Description

This report examines the history of migration patterns in the Afrin region of northwestern Syria, also known as Kurd Dagh, from the Ottoman era through 2010, just before the Syrian Civil War. It highlights how the region's geography and historical context made it a crossroads for various populations and interests. The document outlines the diverse groups who migrated to or through Afrin, including Kurds, Arabs, Circassians, Armenians, and Yazidis, and the varied reasons behind these movements, such as imperial policies, political instability, economic factors, and environmental pressures. The analysis covers key periods like Ottoman rule, emphasizing administrative structures and the settlement of refugees, and the French Mandate, which introduced new borders and further refugee influxes, before concluding with the impact of post-independence Syrian state policies on internal migration and Afrin's demographic makeup. The overall focus is on how these historical processes cumulatively shaped Afrin's predominantly Kurdish identity and complex social fabric by 2010.
