Original Ottoman Text - Exercise

This is the original Ottoman text for the exercises below. Reading this provides "real" Ottoman reading practice.

Congratulations on reading your ninth Ottoman text!

English Translations:

Sentence 1▼
Who is that man? — He is an American gentleman. What is his name? — His name is Mr. Henry Riggs.
Sentence 2▼
Who is this tall (long-statured) foreigner? — He is a gentleman of French nationality.
Sentence 3▼
Who was Cæsar (chézar)? — He was one of the great (emperors) of the ancient Romans.
Sentence 4▼
Where is the city of Rome? — It is in Italy, in the country of the Italians.
Sentence 5▼
Shékérji oghlou (confectioner's son) Ahméd Agha is a stranger; his occupation is that of a confectioner; his father and his grandfather too were sugarmakers; they were of the guild (ésnaf) of confectioners.
Sentence 6▼
The salary of this clerk is two hundred piastres.
Sentence 7▼
The bookseller sells books; the treacleseller sells treacle.
Sentence 8▼
I am not a native of this city, I am a stranger.
Sentence 9▼
Your name is Ali, and my name too is Ali, we two are namesakes.
Sentence 10▼
My lovely house is very small, but I am very poor (There is poverty on my head).
Sentence 11▼
Your brother and I are of the same occupation and age.
Sentence 12▼
The man without money is poor; the man with money is rich.
Sentence 13▼
Although that is a wooded spot, yet it is dry, there is no water.
Sentence 14▼
What is the occupation of your companion? — My companion is a stone-cutter, his father was a baker.
