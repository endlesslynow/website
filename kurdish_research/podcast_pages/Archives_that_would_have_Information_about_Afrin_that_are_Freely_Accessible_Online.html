Description

The provided sources offer a comprehensive guide to researching the history and ongoing conflict in Afrin, Syria, highlighting the fragmented nature of its archival record across various languages and historical periods. They detail specific strategies for accessing information from Ottoman, French Mandate, and contemporary archives, emphasizing the importance of historical nomenclature like "Kurd Dagh" for earlier records. The sources underscore the critical role of human rights and conflict archives in documenting systematic abuses and demographic changes since 2011, while also pointing to the value of visual and cartographic materials for contextualizing historical and current events. Ultimately, the collection stresses the need for a multi-modal research approach to construct a complete narrative of Afrin's complex past and present, alongside an ethical imperative for researchers.
