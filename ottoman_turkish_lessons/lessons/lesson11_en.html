3. Hundreds and Thousands

A hundred, one hundred; a thousand, one thousand are in Turkish simply يوز yûz, بيك biñ. It is not common to say "twelve hundred", but simply "one thousand two hundred".

Examples:

Ottoman TurkishTransliterationTranslation
بيك ايكی يوزbiñ iki yûz1200 (one thousand two hundred)
ايكی بيك بش يوزiki biñ bésh yûz2500 (two thousand five hundred)

4. Financial Terms

For the sum of 100,000 piastres in financial circles the word يوك yûk (load, burden) is used. In olden times 500 piastres was called كيسه késé (bag, purse).

Examples:

Ottoman TurkishTransliterationTranslation
يوكyûkload (100,000 piastres)
كيسهkésépurse (500 piastres)
اون يوكon yûkone million (piastres)
بش كيسه پارهbésh késé para2500 piastres (5 purses)

5. Telling Time

The hours of the day and night are expressed as follows.

Examples (Hours):

Ottoman TurkishTransliterationTranslation
ساعت قاچ در؟Sa'at qach dír?What o'clock is it?
ساعت يارم درSa'at yarîm dir.It is 12:30 o'clock.
ساعت درت درSa'at dêort dûr.It is 4 o'clock.
ساعت يدی بوچوق درSa'at yédi bouchouq dour.It is 7:30 o'clock.

Examples (Minutes):

Ottoman TurkishTransliterationTranslation
بشه اون وارBéshé on' varTen minutes to five.
ايكينی بش كچمشIkiyi bésh' géchmishFive minutes past two.

8. Variative & Approximate Numbers

Variative numerals are formed by adding جنس، جنسدن or چشيد، چشيددن. Approximate numbers use قدر qadar. The word "or" between two numbers is omitted.

Examples:

Ottoman TurkishTransliterationTranslation
بر جنسدنBir jinsdénof one kind
ايكی چشيددنiki chéshid'dénof two kinds
اوتوز قدر، قیرق قدرOtouz qadar, qîrq qadarSome thirty, some forty
اللی آدم قدرEl'li adém qadarSome fifty persons
ايكی اوچ كونIki ûch gûnsome two or three days
بش اون آدم قدرBésh on adém qadarsome five or ten men

11. Counters (Measure Words)

When using a numeral with a noun, Turks frequently introduce a second noun between the two (like "head of cattle").

  • For men: نفر néfér (individual)
  • For beasts: رأس rés (head)
  • For bulbs: باش bash (head)
  • For ships, gardens, fields, letters, maps: قطعه qit'a (piece)
  • For cannons, ships, villages: پاره paré, para
  • For things usually: دانه، عدد dané, tané, adéd

Examples:

Ottoman TurkishTransliterationTranslation
ايكی نفر عسكرiki néfér askértwo soldiers
درت رأس باركيرdêort rés bargirfour pack-horses
اوچ قطعه مكتوبûch qit'a méktoubthree letters
آلتی قطعه تارلاaltî qit'a tarlasix pieces of ground
يدی باش صوغانyédi bash soghanseven bulbs of onions
اون پاره كویon paré kêoyten villages
سكز عدد توفنكsékiz adéd tûféngeight guns
بر باب مغازهbir bab maghazaa magazine (store)
بر قطعه عرضحالbir qita arzouhala petition
