Description

The provided text offers a comprehensive overview of Yarsanism, also known as Ahl-e Haqq or Kaka'i, exploring its history, core beliefs, rituals, social structure, geographical distribution, and its complex relationship with Islam. The sources detail Yarsanism's syncretic origins, key theological concepts like divine manifestation and reincarnation, and important practices such as the jam ceremony and initiation rites. Furthermore, the text examines the challenges and human rights concerns faced by Yarsanis in Iran and Iraq, highlighting issues of discrimination and persecution. Finally, it touches upon cultural expressions and the contemporary resilience of this distinct religious minority.
