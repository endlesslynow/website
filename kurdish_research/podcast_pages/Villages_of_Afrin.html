Villages of Afrin

Description

This document offers an extensive overview of the Afrin District in northwestern Syria prior to 2010. It details the region's geographical features, administrative structure including its seven subdistricts and key towns like Afrin city and Jindires, and provides historical context stretching from ancient times through the Ottoman and French Mandate periods, noting the long-standing Kurdish presence and later Arabization policies. A significant portion focuses on the district's villages, highlighting their historical and archaeological importance, including sites like Ain Dara and those part of the "Dead Cities," alongside their ethno-religious diversity with notable Yazidi, Alevi, Christian, Arab, and Turkmen communities. Finally, the text examines the villages' economic significance, primarily centered on extensive olive cultivation but also including other agricultural activities and local markets.
