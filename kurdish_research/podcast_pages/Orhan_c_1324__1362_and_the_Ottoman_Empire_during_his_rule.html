Orhan c 1324 1362 and the Ottoman Empire during his rule

Description

The provided source details the reign of Orhan Gazi, the second Ottoman ruler, emphasizing his pivotal role in transforming the nascent Ottoman beylik into an organized state poised for empire. It explains how Orhan, through a combination of military conquests like the capture of Bursa and Gallipoli, and shrewd diplomacy including strategic marriages, significantly expanded Ottoman territory and influence, particularly by establishing a foothold in Europe. The text highlights his essential institutional reforms, such as creating a standing army, minting the first Ottoman currency, and establishing a formal administration and urban development, arguing that these foundational contributions make him the true architect of the Ottoman Empire. Furthermore, it explores Orhan's dual persona as a Ghazi warrior and pragmatic statesman, whose vision laid the groundwork for future Ottoman power.
