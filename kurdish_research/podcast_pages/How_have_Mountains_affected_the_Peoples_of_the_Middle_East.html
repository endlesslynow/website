How have Mountains affected the Peoples of the Middle East

Download Audio File

Description

This comprehensive text examines the profound influence of mountains on the Middle East and its diverse inhabitants before 2010. It explores how ranges like the Zagros, Taurus, and Elburz shaped everything from the earliest settlements and agricultural practices to the development of unique cultural identities and languages by serving as both barriers and pathways. The sources detail the crucial roles mountains played as sanctuaries for minority groups, strategic points for defense and warfare, and sources of vital resources like water, timber, and minerals. Furthermore, the text discusses how these towering landscapes became deeply embedded in regional mythologies and religious beliefs, highlighting their significance as sacred sites and abodes of the divine, ultimately illustrating the enduring legacies of these vertical worlds on the region's history and peoples.
