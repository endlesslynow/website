What was the Ethnic Makeup of Afrin in its Hellenistic and Roman Periods

Description

The provided text examines the ethnic composition of the Afrin region, known historically as Cyrrhestica, throughout the Hellenistic and Roman periods. It outlines how the region, initially populated by indigenous Aramaic-speaking peoples, experienced significant cultural and demographic shifts due to successive imperial controls. The document details the establishment of Greek-speaking cities and military settlements under the Seleucids, followed by the multi-ethnic influx of Roman legions and administrators. Evidence from linguistic analysis, funerary practices, and material culture reveals a complex society where indigenous traditions persisted alongside adopted Hellenistic and Roman identities, creating a vibrant cultural mosaic.
