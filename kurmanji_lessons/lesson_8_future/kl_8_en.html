2. Future Tense

We form simple future tense by using the future marker ê after the subject, and before the verb, and conjugating the verb with the subjunctive bi-.

Note: Think of the prefix "bi-" as showing something that doesn't completely exist in reality. It often shows verbs that have a potential of happening or will happen in the future. But they're not real yet!

Note II: Not just ê is used as the future marker. Also dê, kê, and wê can be used, depending on the dialect. Interestingly, many Kurds outside of Afrin will refuse to believe that kê is used as the future marker in the Afrini dialect.

Structure of the Future Tense:

StructureExampleTranslation
Subject + ê + verb (with bi-)Ez ê biçim.I will go.

Future Tense Conjugation:

KurmanjiEnglish
Ez ê biçim.I will go.
Tu ê biçî.You will go.
Ew ê biçe.He/She will go.
Em ê biçin.We will go.
Hûn ê biçin.You (pl) will go.
Ew ê biçin.They will go.

Examples:

Ez ê sibehê biçim nexweşxaneyê. (I will go to the hospital tomorrow.)

Tu ê kengî bêyî mala me? (When will you come to our home?)

Ew ê çawa we nas bikin? (How will they know you?)

Exercise 1: Complete with the Correct Future Tense Form

3. Negative Future Tense

To form the negative future tense in Kurmanji, the future marker ê is dropped, and na replaces the subjunctive bi-.

KurmanjiEnglish
Ez naçim.I will not go.
Tu naçî.You will not go.
Ew naçe.He/She will not go.
Em naçin.We will not go.
Hûn naçin.You (pl) will not go.
Ew naçin.They will not go.

Examples:

Ez ê sibehê biçim nexweşxaneyê. (I will go to the hospital tomorrow.)

Tu ê kengî bêyî mala me? (When will you come to our home?)

Em naçin dibistanê. (We will not go to school.)

Ew naxwin goşt. (They will not eat meat.)

Exercise 2: Fill in the Correct Negative Future Form

Ez sibehê dibistanê .(I will not go to school tomorrow.)
Ew goşt .(They will not eat meat.)
Hûn tiştekî .(You will not buy anything.)
Em êdî tu car wê .(We will never see her again.)
Tu sibe ?(Will you not work tomorrow?)

4. Future Tense Usage

The future tense in Kurmanji is used in similar situations as in English, such as:

Making Predictions:

Examples:

Ew ê bibe doktor. (He/She will become a doctor.)

Hûn ê bibînin, hemû tişt ê baş bibin. (You will see, everything will be good.)

Making Plans:

Examples:

Ez ê sibehê herim Amedê. (I will go to Amed tomorrow.)

Em ê îşev fîlmekî bibînin. (We will watch a movie tonight.)

Making Promises:

Examples:

Ez ê alîkariya te bikim. (I will help you.)

Ew ê bi te re werin. (They will come with you.)

5. Comparative Forms

To yield the comparative form of adjectives we:

  1. add the –tir suffix to the positive form (the first form) of the adjective
  2. use the preposition ji 'from' before the compared noun

However, if the last letter of the adjective is already t, then we drop the t of the comparative suffix.

Regular Comparatives:

Positive FormComparative FormEnglish
mezin (big)mezintirbigger
baş (good)baştirbetter
biha (expensive)bihatirmore expensive
dirêj (long)dirêjtirlonger
lez (fast)leztirfaster

Examples:

Meyrem ji birayê xwe mezintir e. (Meyrem is older than her brother.)

Ev cil û berg ji yên modern bihatir in. (These clothes are more expensive than the modern ones.)

Hevokên min ji yên te dirêjtir in. (My sentences are longer than yours.)

Tu ji min baştir dixebitî. (You work better than me.)

Ew ji me hemiyan leztir dibezin. (They run faster than all of us.)

More Comparatives:

Positive FormComparative FormEnglish
pir (much)pirtir or bêtirmore
baş (good)baştir or çêtirbetter
mezin (big)mezintir or meztirbigger

Note: Although there is a way in Kurmanji to say a superlative (the most, the biggest), this concept doesn't seem to exist beyond textbooks, or it's very unimportant, in the Kurdish cultures I've been familiar with. Instead, simply put a word for "very" in front of what you're talking about.

Example: Ev pir xweş e. (This is very nice.) instead of "This is the nicest."

Likewise, there's no importance placed on things that are ranked "the highest". For example, you wouldn't say something is "my favourite". You'd just say, "I like it a lot."

Example: Ez ji vê pir hez dikim. (I like this a lot.) instead of "This is my favorite."

Exercise 4: Form the Comparative

Ev mal ji mala wî e.(This house is more expensive than his house.)
Bajarê wan ji bajarê me e.(Their city is bigger than our city.)
Ev xwarin ji ya duh e.(This food is better than yesterday's.)
Porê wê ji yê te e.(Her hair is longer than yours.)
Min ji te xebitî.(I worked more than you.)

6. Expressing Preferences

In Kurmanji, we can use comparative forms to express preferences:

Examples:

Ez bêtir ji çayê hez dikim (I like tea more.)

Birayê min ji min mezintir e. (My brother is older than me.)

Ez pir ji vê pirtûkê hez dikim. (I like this book a lot.)

7. Conditionals

The conditional is formed by using one of the words heke, ku, ger, eger, heger (all meaning if) at the beginning of the sentence and conjugating the verb in the present subjunctive form, as in future tense. For the present lesson we will focus only on the conditionals in the present tense. We will prefer heke conditional word as it is the most frequent word for forming conditional sentences.

Structure of Present Conditional:

Conditional WordMeaning
Heke, ku, ger, eger, hegerif

Present Conditional - Affirmative:

KurmanjiEnglish
Heke ez biçim.If I go.

Present Conditional - Negative:

As for the negative form of the present conditional, we simply replace the subjunctive bi- with the negation marker ne- and keep the rest untouched.

KurmanjiEnglish
Heke ez neçim.If I do not go.

Examples:

Heke zarokên me kurdî fêr nebin, ê xetereke mezin çêbe. (If our children do not learn Kurdish, there will be a big danger.)

Heke hûn biçin bazarê, hûn dikarin tiştên gelek erzan peyda bikin. (If you go to bazaar, you can find very cheap things.)

Heke ez ne xelet bim, ew li Stenbolê dixwîne. (If I am not wrong, he studies in Istanbul.)

Exercise 6: Complete the Conditional Sentences

Heke tu dibistanê , dê tu tiştan fêr bibî.(If you go to school, you will learn things.)
Heke tu , tu nabî dewlemend.(If you don't work, you won't become rich.)
Heke ew pirtûkê , dê zanyariya wan zêde bibe.(If they read the book, their knowledge will increase.)
Ger ez Kurdistanê , ez ê gelek bajaran bibînim.(If I go to Kurdistan, I will see many cities.)
Heke baranê , em naçin derve.(If it rains, we will not go outside.)

9. Dialogue

Read this dialogue about future plans and comparisons:

Azad: Sibe tu dê çi bikî?
What will you do tomorrow?
Rojîn: Ez dê biçim bazarê. Divê ez hinekî tiştan bikîrim.
I will go to the market. I need to buy some things.
Azad: Tu dê hevalên xwe jî bibînî?
Will you see your friends too?
Rojîn: Na, ez bawer nakim. Bazara nû ji ya kevin mezintir e.
No, I don't think so. The new market is bigger than the old one.
Azad: Lê tiştên li wir bihatir in, ne wisa?
But things there are more expensive, aren't they?
Rojîn: Belê, lê kalîteya wan jî baştir e.
Yes, but their quality is better too.
Azad: Heke tu sibê biçî bazarê, ez jî dê bi te re werim.
If you go to the market tomorrow, I will come with you.
Rojîn: Baş e. Em dikarin li kafeyekê çayekê vexwin.
Good. We can drink tea at a café.
Azad: Ez ji çayê bêtir hez dikim, lê tu dizanî ez ji qehweyê jî pir hez dikim.
I like tea more, but you know I like coffee a lot too.
Rojîn: Heke em biçin kafeyê, em dikarin hem çay hem jî qehweyê vexwin.
If we go to the café, we can drink both tea and coffee.
Azad: Wê demê, ez dê saet dehê li ber mala te bim.
In that case, I will be in front of your house at ten o'clock.
