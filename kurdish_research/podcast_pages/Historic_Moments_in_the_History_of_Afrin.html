Historic Moments in the History of Afrin

Download Audio File

Description

These sources provide a historical overview of the Afrin region in northwestern Syria before 2010. They trace its deep roots back to ancient civilizations like the Hellenistic, Roman, and Byzantine periods through the establishment of cities like Cyrrhus. The text highlights the region's integration into various empires, including the Muslim and Ottoman empires, and the consolidation of its distinct Kurdish identity and social structure. Crucially, the sources emphasize the transformative impact of 20th-century geopolitical shifts, particularly the demarcation of the Syria-Turkey border and Turkey's annexation of Hatay, which geographically isolated Afrin. Finally, the document examines the period of Ba'ath rule, characterized by the marginalization of Kurdish identity despite the area avoiding the most severe Arabization policies seen elsewhere.
