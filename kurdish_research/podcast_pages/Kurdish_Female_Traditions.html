Kurdish Female Traditions

Download Audio File

Description

These sources present an ethnographic report on the multifaceted traditions of Kurdish women, spanning diverse geographies and historical periods. They explore the complex interplay of cultural continuity and change, highlighting the persistent tension between patriarchal structures and female agency across social, cultural, and religious spheres. The report examines traditional aspects like family roles, rites of passage, clothing, and crafts, while also detailing the impact of modernity and political movements on women's roles. It further discusses the varied religious and spiritual traditions of Kurdish women within Islam, Yazidism, Yarsanism, and Alevism, and underscores their significant historical and contemporary contributions through leadership, activism, and cultural expression. Finally, the text addresses the ongoing challenges faced by Kurdish women and their remarkable resilience and evolving traditions both in their homeland and the diaspora.
