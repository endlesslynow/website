Description

These sources comprehensively introduce Manichaeism, a syncretic world religion founded by Mani in the 3rd century CE, highlighting its core dualistic tenets of Light and Darkness and salvation through gnosis. They explore the life and revelations of Mani, his missionary efforts, and eventual martyrdom, which became central to the faith's narrative. The texts also detail Manichaean cosmogony, soteriology, and eschatology, explaining the cosmic struggle and the path to liberation for the soul. Furthermore, they discuss Manichaeism's absorption of elements from Zoroastrianism, Christianity, Gnosticism, and Buddhism, as well as the structure of the Manichaean church, its rituals, sacred texts, and remarkable geographical spread along the Silk Road. Finally, the sources cover the religion's eventual decline due to persecution and internal challenges, its legacy in later dualistic movements, and the profound impact of modern archaeological discoveries on current scholarship of this once-global faith.
