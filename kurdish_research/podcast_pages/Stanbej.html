Description

This report focuses on the Stranbêj, a significant figure in Kurdish oral tradition and musical heritage. It clarifies that the correct term is Stranbêj, literally meaning "singer of songs," and distinguishes this role from other Kurdish oral performers like dengbêj (bards) and çîrokbêj (storytellers). The document examines the cultural importance of the Stranbêj, detailing their function as preservers of language and memory, their diverse musical repertoire and performance contexts, and their historical evolution through challenging periods of political adversity and censorship. Finally, it explores the scholarly perspectives on the Stranbêj and their place within broader comparative oral traditions.
