Explorers who passed through the Afrin region before the French Mandate Period

Download Audio File

Description

This report provides an overview of European explorers and travelers who visited or documented the geographical area encompassing modern-day Afrin in northwestern Syria before the French Mandate (circa 1920). It explains that the region was historically known by names like Kurd-Dagh (Kurd Mountains) and the valley of the Afrin River, centered around the ancient city of Cyrrhus (Nebi Huri). From early accounts in the late 16th century noting the presence of Kurds, the text traces increasing European attention driven by various interests like trade, archaeology, and geopolitics. The report highlights key explorers and their observations on the landscape, inhabitants (including Kurds and Yezidis), and archaeological sites like Cyrrhus, demonstrating the growing, though often biased, external understanding of this area within the Ottoman context prior to significant 20th-century changes.
