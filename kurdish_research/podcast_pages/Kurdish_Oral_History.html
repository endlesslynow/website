Description

The provided text, "The Living Archive: An Exploration of Kurdish Oral History," examines the multifaceted nature and significance of Kurdish oral traditions. It highlights how these traditions have served as vital repositories of culture, history, and identity for the stateless Kurdish nation, especially in the face of linguistic and cultural suppression. The report explores various forms of oral expression, including myths, epics, storytelling, and the roles of key figures like the Dengbêj (bard) and Zarbêj (female oral creator) in their transmission. Furthermore, it discusses contemporary challenges to preserving this heritage due to political conflict, modernization, and the complexities of archiving, while also outlining ongoing revitalization efforts and the "archival turn" in Kurdish studies.
