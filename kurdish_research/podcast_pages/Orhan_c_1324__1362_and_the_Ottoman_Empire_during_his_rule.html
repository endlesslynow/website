Orhan c 1324 1362 and the Ottoman Empire during his rule

Description

The provided text examines the reign of Orhan Gazi, portraying him as the foundational architect of the Ottoman Empire who transformed a small principality into a formidable state between 1324 and 1362. It explores his personal character and dynastic strategies, including his politically significant marriages and the critical succession challenges that shaped the early empire. The sources also detail his strategic conquests in Anatolia and the Balkans, notably the capture of Bursa and Gallipoli, and illuminate the revolutionary military reforms he implemented, such as the creation of a standing army and the genesis of the Janissary corps. Furthermore, the text highlights his establishment of core governmental institutions, including the Divan and vizierate, the minting of the first Ottoman currency (the akçe), and the pragmatic policy of *istimalet* to govern diverse populations. Ultimately, it argues that Orhan's foresight laid the essential groundwork for the empire's enduring power and expansion into Europe.
