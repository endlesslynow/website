Suleiman II (1687 – 1691) and the Ottoman Empire during his rule

Download Audio File

Description

The source provides an extensive historical analysis of the four-year reign of Sultan Suleiman II of the Ottoman Empire, which lasted from 1687 to 1691, a period marked by profound crisis. The text details how Suleiman II ascended the throne after nearly forty years of seclusion in the Kafes (the imperial cage), making him severely unprepared yet deeply pious and duty-bound. Facing military disaster during the Great Turkish War and complete internal anarchy, the Sultan’s most crucial act was empowering the highly competent Köprülü Fazıl Mustafa Pasha as Grand Vizier with complete authority to enact radical reforms. These reforms included a complete overhaul of the tax system, the purge of corrupt officials, and a successful military counter-offensive that culminated in the recapture of Belgrade in 1690. Ultimately, this temporary stabilization effort collapsed with the death of Fazıl Mustafa Pasha in battle in 1691, highlighting the systemic fragility of the empire despite the proven resilience and exceptional individual leadership.
