Abdulmejid I (1839 – 1861) and the Ottoman Empire during his rule

Download Audio File

Description

The source provides a detailed historical overview of the reign of Sultan Abdulmejid I (1839–1861) and the foundational period of the Tanzimat reforms in the Ottoman Empire. It analyzes the complex, contradictory character of the Sultan—a liberal Francophile who was also an easily manipulated monarch—and argues that his weakness allowed powerful bureaucrats to implement sweeping modernization. The text highlights how the major reforms, codified in the Edicts of Gülhane (1839) and Hatt-ı Hümayun (1856), were largely motivated by the need for European support during crises like the Egyptian invasion and the Crimean War. Ultimately, the reforms presented a paradox: they structurally strengthened the central state but simultaneously led to disastrous side effects, including the onset of foreign debt and the intensification of nationalist movements, ensuring the Empire's eventual decline.
