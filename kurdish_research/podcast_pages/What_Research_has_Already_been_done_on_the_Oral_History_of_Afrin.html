What Research has Already been done on the Oral History of Afrin

Description

These sources emphasize that oral history is essential for understanding Afrin, a region in northern Syria with a strong Kurdish heritage that has faced significant conflict and displacement. They explain that traditional oral literature, such as epics and folk tales in the Kurmanji dialect, is a key part of Afrin's cultural memory, transmitted by storytellers and singers. Furthermore, the texts show that oral accounts of life before 2018 depict a period of peace centered around olive cultivation. Crucially, contemporary testimonies document the trauma of the 2018 invasion, including human rights abuses and the destruction of land, while also highlighting community resilience and efforts to preserve identity through storytelling and cultural practices. The sources reveal that documentation efforts are ongoing, involving researchers, cultural associations, and human rights groups using various platforms to ensure these histories are not lost.
