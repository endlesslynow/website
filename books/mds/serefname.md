4. Ez, ku nivîskarê van rûpelan im, navê min Şerefê kurê Şemseddin e.
4. I, who am the author of these pages, my name is Şeref son of Şemseddin.

5. Çavê min li meziniya Xwedê ye û ez hêvîdar im ku alîkarê min be û min di vê dinyayê de serfiraz bike û ji asteng û giriyên wê dinyaya din jî rizgar bike.
5. My eyes are on the greatness of God and I am hopeful that He will be my helper and make me triumphant in this world and save me from the obstacles and sorrows of the other world as well.

6. Di kemilîna ciwanî û gulvedan û ajardana bihara jiyana xwe de, piştî ku min destê xwe yê rast û destê çepê nas kir, min di xwendewariyê de hêz û taqet bi dest xist û ez bi hezaran milên zanistiya zahirî û batinî hîn bûm.
6. In the maturity of youth and the blooming and sprouting of the spring of my life, after I recognized my right hand from my left hand, I gained strength and capability in literacy and I learned thousands of branches of external and internal science.

7. Çi tiştên ji bo dîn û dinyaya min pêwîst e, min ji her tiştî gelek zanîbû û bi sererastî û meharetî ketim ser kar û barên dîwanê û wê çaxê heta niha hemû bi hêz û mecalên xwe ve ez bi xwendina serpêhatî û dîrokan mijûl im.
7. Whatever things are necessary for my religion and my world, I knew a lot about everything and with correctness and skill I engaged in the works and affairs of the court, and from that time until now with all my strength and means I have been busy with reading adventures and histories.

8. ew kesên di bazara nivîsandinê de cewherfiroş û durrnas in, ew dîrokzanên ku lehengên nav şekerîstana serpêhatî, çîrok, deng û behsan in, heta niha riya xwe ber bi Kurdistanê ve nekirine, behs û dengên gernas û sernasên Kurdan nekirine bin devê pênûsê, ew dîroka gelek bi şanazî û xweşiyê ku di tam û bîhna xwe de wek hingivîn û gezoyê ne, gelek mixabin ji aliyê wan ve nayê naskirin û ketiye bin piyan û bi tu awayî dest û devên xwe lê nêzîk nekirine.
8. Those people who are jewelers and pearl-connoisseurs in the market of writing, those historians who are the heroes in the sugar-field of adventures, tales, voices and accounts, have until now not made their way toward Kurdistan, have not put the accounts and voices of the heroes and famous figures of the Kurds under the mouth of the pen; that history so full of pride and pleasantness which in its taste and smell is like honey and manna, is very unfortunately not recognized by them and has fallen underfoot, and they have in no way brought their hands and mouths near it.

9. Ez i jar, bêtaqet, bêdest û ziman ketim ser wê xeyalê ku vê mijara han a ji taqet û qudreta min gelek der; behs û serpêhatiyên mirovên mezin, kesên bi nav û deng, serdar û fermanrewayên Kurd û Kurdistanê berhev bikim, ên di dîrokên 'Ecem de min bi xwe dîtine û yên min ji mirovên bi hiş û zîrek û ji derewan dûr, raste rast bi xwe seh kirine, binivîsim û navê wê deynim: ŞEREFNAME.
9. I, poor, powerless, without hands or tongue, fell upon the thought that this matter which is far beyond my power and strength; to compile the accounts and adventures of great people, famous persons, commanders and rulers of the Kurds and Kurdistan, those which I have seen myself in the histories of the 'Ecem (Persians) and those which I have directly heard myself from intelligent and sharp people who are far from lies, I should write down and give it the name: ŞEREFNAME.

10. Hemû armanc û daxwaza min jî ev e ku, xanedanên mezin ên Kurdistanê navê wan di nav navan de bimîne û ji bîr neçe.
10. All my aim and desire is this, that the names of the great dynasties of Kurdistan remain among the names and are not forgotten.

The owner of this manuscript as well has written thus at the end of the book: "With the help and grace of the forgiving God, the most poor slave under your hand Mehmûd Riza the son of Sabir 'Eli of Karbala, from the village of Dîlmekan, on Tuesday near breakfast, the 19th of the month of Sha'ban, finished the manuscript of this book."

224. This manuscript of Khanikov is 250 pages.

225. Nusxeya sisiyan a bi destnivîs ku V. Zirnov li ser xebitiye, ew destnivîsa li Muzexaneya Asyayê ya Universîteya Qeyser ya li St. Petersburgê ye.
225. The third handwritten copy which V. Zirnov worked on, is that manuscript in the Asian Museum of the Imperial University in St. Petersburg.

227. This came as a gift to Rosso.

228. Ew kesê xwediyê wê ku diyarî aniye, li ser rûpela yekê wiha nivîsandiye: "Ji Bada Xanê Hakimê Sawucbulax?"
228. That person who was its owner who brought the gift, has written thus on the first page: "From Bada Xan the Ruler of Sawucbulax?"

229. Lê ne tarîxa nivîsandina wê û ne jî navê nivîskarê wê li sere.
229. But neither its date of writing nor the name of its scribe is on it.

230. Ev nusxeya han a Rosso ji birêz M. Wolkow re hatiye dayîn û wî jî maqaleyek li ser nivîsandiye û di rojnameya Asya de daye weşandin.
230. This copy of Rosso was given to Mr. M. Wolkow and he also wrote an article on it and had it published in the Asia journal.

231. Nusxeya bi destnivîs a çaran ku V. Zirnov jê îstîfade kiriye, ew nusxeya di Muzexaneya Asyayê ya di bin reqema 576'an de qeyîtkiriye.
231. The fourth handwritten copy from which V. Zirnov utilized, is that copy registered in the Asian Museum under the number 576.

232. Ev nusxeya han ji aliyê Baron Bond ve ji Îranê hatiye anîn û 100 rûpel e.
232. This copy was brought from Iran by Baron Bond and is 100 pages.

233. As we also said above, V. Zirnov brought all four of these copies together and formed one copy out of them.

234. Yanî ew nusxeya ku di sala 1860'an de li St. Petersburgê bi Farisî, yanî zimanê kitêbê yê orijinal hatiye çapkirin.
234. That is, that copy which was printed in the year 1860 in St. Petersburg in Persian, meaning the original language of the book.

235. Bi gotineke din ew nusxeya ku "çapa Rûsyaye" jî jê re tê gotin.
235. In other words, that copy which is also called the "Russian edition".

236. The Persian edition of Egypt was also prepared from these three copies: The first copy, that copy of V. Zirnov which we discussed above, that is the "Russian edition" copy.

237. Ya duduyan, nusxeyek destnivîs a di Kitêbxaneya Mekteba Osmani ya Helebê de ye, ku ji aliyê M. 'Elî 'Ewnî Beg ve hatiye dîtin.
237. The second, is a handwritten copy in the Library of the Ottoman School in Aleppo, which was seen by M. 'Elî 'Ewnî Beg.

238. Nusxeya sisiyan jî, wekî ku M. 'Elî 'Ewnî Beg di pêşgotina çapa Misrê de dibêje, ji aliyê Sureyya Bedirxan Beg ve jê re hatiye dayîn.
238. And the third copy, as M. 'Elî 'Ewnî Beg says in the preface to the Egyptian edition, was given to him by Sureyya Bedirxan Beg.

239. Again according to the word of M. 'Elî 'Ewnî Beg, Sureyya Bedirxan gave him the list of 170 books about the Kurds and Kurdistan and their places and years of printing along with it, through two indexes, but he says that, very unfortunately, we could not publish it with the Persian edition.

240. Ev çapa Şerefnameyê ya Misrê, cardin wekî ku M. 'Elî 'Ewnî Beg dibêje, ne tenê ji aliyê wî ve hatiye kirin.
240. This Egyptian edition of the Şerefname, again as M. 'Elî 'Ewnî Beg says, was not done only by him.

241. Wî, Şêx Ferecûllah Zekî Kurdî û Şêx Muhyeddîn Sebrî Kurdî di navbera xwe de ew xebat par ve kirine û piştî xebateke salekî ya bê rawestan ew ji çapê re amade kirine.
241. He, Şêx Ferecûllah Zekî Kurdî and Şêx Muhyeddîn Sebrî Kurdî divided that work among themselves and after a year of non-stop work they prepared it for print.

242. Li gor Dr. Kemal Mezher Ehmed, hejmara destnivîsên Şerefnameyê yên di kitêbxaneyên naskirî yên cihê cihê de ku hatine parastin, digihîjin 22 liban.
242. According to Dr. Kemal Mezher Ehmed, the number of manuscripts of the Şerefname which have been preserved in various known libraries, reach 22 pieces.

243. But the people who have done research on the Şerefname, are of the belief

244. that if searched, many other handwritten copies can be obtained in Kurdistan.

245. Niha ez dixwazim çend gotin jî li ser vê wergera Kurdî ya bi zaravayê Kurmancî bêjim.
245. Now I wish to say a few words as well regarding this Kurdish translation in the Kurmanji dialect.

246. Teqrîben pênc sal berî niha, ji bo çarsedsaliya temambûna nivîsandina Şerefnameyê fikra wergera wê ya bi ser Kurdî ya bi zaravayê Kurmancî ket serê min.
246. Approximately five years ago, for the four-hundredth anniversary of the completion of the writing of the Şerefname, the idea of translating it into Kurdish in the Kurmanji dialect fell into my head.

247. Wê çaxê wergera Mam Hejar a bi zaravayê Soranî nû ketibû destê min.
247. At that time the translation of Mam Hejar in the Sorani dialect had just fallen into my hand.

248. Ji bo ku wergera Tirkî ji Erebiya wê hatibû kirin, zimanek ketibû navberê û di wergerê de jî ev xalekî girîng e, ev wergera Kurdî ya zaravayê Soranî ez teşwîqê vî karî kirim.
248. Since the Turkish translation had been made from its Arabic, a language had fallen in between, and in translation this is also an important point; this Kurdish translation of the Sorani dialect encouraged me to do this work.

249. Wergera Şerefnameyê ya bi zaravayê Soranî, ji aliyê Mam Hejar ve raste rast ji çapa Farisî ya Misrê hatiye kirin.
249. The translation of the Şerefname in the Sorani dialect was done by Mam Hejar directly from the Persian edition of Egypt.

250. Dema ku mirov li vê prosesa wergerê binêre û nêzîkahiya di navbera zaravayê Kurmancî û Soranî jî bigre ber çav, mirov dikare wergerek wiha ya Kurmanciya Şerefnameyê, wekê ya ji ber orijinala wê bi xwe hatî çêkirin bihesibîne.
250. When one looks at this process of translation and also takes into consideration the closeness between the Kurmanji and Sorani dialects, one can consider such a Kurmanji translation of the Şerefname as one that was made from its original itself.

251. Lê, dema min dest bi vî karî kir û ez pê de çûm, neqla bûyerên wekî yên Buxduz (Baxduz), ew teswîr û sifetên ji bo pesin û serkevtina Kurdan hatine bikaranîn, ji min re dudilî çêbû û ez ketim şikê.
251. But, when I began this work and I went forward with it, the transfer of events such as those of Buxduz (Baxduz), those descriptions and adjectives used for the praise and victory of the Kurds, caused hesitation for me and I fell into doubt.

252. Ji ber vê, ji bo ku ez bikarim vê xebata han bi awakî rêk û pêkî bimeşînim, ez mecbur mam min serî li wergera Tirkî da.
252. Because of this, so that I could carry out this work in an orderly and proper manner, I was obliged to resort to the Turkish translation.

253. Min wergera Tirkî û wergera Kurdî ya zaravayê Soranî, ez dikarim bêjim ku ji serî heta binî dan ber hev.
253. The Turkish translation and the Kurdish translation of the Sorani dialect, I can say that from top to bottom I compared them with each other.

254. Ew ciyên ku li hev nedikirin û ferqiyetên mezin di navbera wan de hebûn, min ew işaret kirin û bi alîkariya hevalên Farisîzan, ew bi orijînalên wan yên Farisî ya ji aliyê Mihemmed 'Ebbasî ve hatî amadekirin, muqayese kirin.
254. Those places which did not match and there were great differences between them, I marked them and with the help of friends who knew Persian, compared them with their Persian originals prepared by Mihemmed 'Ebbasî.

255. Bi vî awayî ew şaşiyên ku di herdu wergeran de jî hebûn, min ew di jêrnotan de dan xuyakirin.
255. In this way, those errors which existed in both translations, I pointed them out in the footnotes.

256. Mam Hejar, ku me li çend ciyan di jêrnotan de îşaret kiriye, li gelek ciyan rîayetê bikaranîna termînolojiya îdarî û siyasî ya wê çaxê nekiriye.
256. Mam Hejar, as we have indicated in several places in the footnotes, in many places did not adhere to the use of the administrative and political terminology of that time.

257. Li gelek ciyan jî ew teswîr û pesnên ku ji bo Kurd û Kurdistanê hatine kirin, gelek dirêj kiriye.
257. In many places as well he made those descriptions and praises that were done for the Kurds and Kurdistan very long.

258. Ji ber vê, daneberheviya herdu wergeran di ciyên wiha de zehmet bû.
258. Because of this, the comparison of both translations in such places was difficult.

259. Ji bilî vê, di wergera şi'iran a bi ser zaravayê Soranî de gelek têkelhevî hene.
259. Besides this, in the translation of the poems into the Sorani dialect there are many mix-ups.

260. Hinek ciyan Mam Hejar di ciyê wergera şi'iran de, şi'irên xwe bi xwe nivîsandine.
260. In some places Mam Hejar, instead of translating the poems, has written his own poems.

261. Lê ji derveyî şi'iran, bi gelemperî ew ciyên ku di herdu wergeran de li hev nedikirin û me bi saya orjinala wê ya Farisî rast kirin, hat dîtin ku di wergerê de Mam Hejar zêdetir nêzîkî me'neyê bûye.
261. But outside of the poems, generally those places which did not match in the two translations and we corrected thanks to its Persian original, it was seen that in the translation Mam Hejar was closer to the meaning.

262. Me tev ev xalên han di jêrnotan de dane xuyakirin.
262. We have pointed out all these points in the footnotes.

263. Problemek din ku derket pêşiya me, mesela salên koçî û wergera wan bi ser salên zayînî bûn.
263. Another problem that appeared before us, was the issue of the Hijri years and their translation to Gregorian years.

264. Ji bo lîsteyek ku mirov bikare pê bawer be bi dest
264. Because a list that one could trust could not be

265. neket, me salên wan ên zayînî wekî yên wergera Tirkî qebûl kirin.
265. obtained, we accepted their Gregorian years as they were in the Turkish translation.

266. Ev salên zayînî yên wergera Tirkî, gelek jê ji aliyê M. 'Elî 'Ewnî Beg ve hatine kirin û yên din jî Seydayê M. Emîn Bozarslan wergerandine.
266. These Gregorian years of the Turkish translation, many of them were done by M. 'Elî 'Ewnî Beg and the others were translated by Seyda M. Emîn Bozarslan.

267. Me ew pêşgotinên ku ji aliyê kesên cihê cihê ve hatine nivîsandin, kirin pêşiya kitêbê, hakeza ew belgeyên bi destê me ketin û qapaxên wergerên bi zimanê din jî me avêtin dawiya kitêbê.
267. We put those prefaces which were written by various persons at the front of the book, likewise those documents that fell into our hand and the covers of the translations in other languages as well we threw (placed) at the end of the book.

268. Ez dixwazim ev werger ji aliyê xwendevanên hêja ve ji bo çarsedsaliya temambûna nivîsandina berhemeke weke Şerefnameyê re diyariyek biçûk, berê ked û zehmetekî hindik bê qebulkirin.
268. I want this translation to be accepted by the esteemed readers as a small gift for the four-hundredth anniversary of the completion of the writing of a work like the Şerefname, the product of a little toil and difficulty.

269. Qet nebe, dengeki jar jî be hewarek ji vê bêdengiyê re ye.
269. At the very least, even if it is a weak voice, it is a cry out against this silence.

270. Ez hêvîdar im ku rojekê muesseseyên Kurdan jî çêbin û kesên zimanzan û dîroknas peyda bibin û bên ba hev, ew destnivîs û xebatên li jorê behsa wan hatin kirin, bi taybetî nusxeya yekê ku bi destxeta Şeref Xan bi xwe ye û ew pêşgotinên ji hemû wergerên cihê cihê re hatine nivîsandin bigrin ber çavan û careke din ji nû de wergera Şerefnameyê têkin ber destê xwendevanan û Şerefnameya Mîr Şeref Xanê Bedlîsî nedin jibîrkirin.
270. I am hopeful that one day Kurdish institutions will also be established and linguists and historians will be found and come together, they will take into consideration those manuscripts and works that were discussed above, especially the first copy which is in Şeref Xan's own handwriting and those prefaces that have been written for all the various translations, and once again anew put the translation of the Şerefname into the hands of the readers and not let the Şerefname of Mîr Şeref Xanê Bedlîsî be forgotten.

271. Ez di dawiyê de sipasî hemû wan kesan dikim ku alîkariya vê xebata han kirin.
271. I finally thank all those people who helped with this work.

272. Ez gelek şad û dilxweş im ku erkê wergera afirandineke evqas hêja, bi kil û kêmasiyên xwe ve jî be ket ser milê min.
272. I am very glad and happy that the duty of translating such a worthy creation, even with its flaws and deficiencies, fell upon my shoulders.

273. Bi hêviya ku bi dilê xwendevanên hêja be û bi kêrî wan were.
273. With the hope that it will be to the heart's liking of the esteemed readers and be of use to them.

275. ## SUMMARY OF MAM HEJAR'S PREFACE TO THE SORANI TRANSLATION

276. Ez zarok bûm û hê temenê min ji bihara xwe ya çardehan derbas nebûbû.
276. I was a child and my age had not yet passed its fourteenth spring.

277. Di xwendinê de jî hê min gavên mezin ên bi xurt neavêtibûn, ku cara yekê li ser refên dîwanxaneya Qerenî Axayê Mameş kitêbek li ber çavê min ket û li ser pişta wê hatibû nivîsandin: "Şerefname"
277. In reading as well I still had not taken strong big steps, when for the first time on the shelves of the guesthouse of Qerenî Axa of Mameş a book caught my eye and on its spine was written: "Şerefname"

278. Min çend rûpel jê zivirand û li vî milî û li wî milî nêrî, tenê ez têgihîştim ku dîrok e, min tiştek jê dernexist.
278. I turned a few pages of it and looked at this side and that side, I only understood that it was history, I got nothing out of it.

279. Sal hatin û sal çûn, car û caran min li ha û li wir seh dikir ku Şeref Xan li ser filan bûyerê wiha gotiye û Şerefnameyê bi vî awayî behsa vê serpêhatiyê kiriye.
279. Years came and years went, from time to time I would hear here and there that Şeref Xan has spoken thus about such-and-such event and the Şerefname has in this way discussed this adventure.

280. Li ser vê, ez nû pê hesiyam ew Şerefnameya ku min tiştekî jê fehm nekiribû, ew dîroka Kurd û Kurdistanê ye û Kurdekî gelperwer û xwedî huner bi zimanê Farisî nivîsandiye.
280. Upon this, I newly realized that the Şerefname from which I had understood nothing, is the history of the Kurds and Kurdistan and a patriotic and artistic Kurd had written it in the Persian language.

281. Hatiye wergerandin bi ser gelek zimanan û du caran jî ji aliyê doktorê zana û dilsoz ve Erebiya wê hatiye çêkirin.
281. It has been translated into many languages and twice as well its Arabic has been made by a knowledgeable and loyal doctor.

282. Yanî ew dîroka Kurd a herî hêja, zimanê Kurdî nebe bi hemû zimanan dest dikeve...
282. That is, that most worthy Kurdish history, if not the Kurdish language, could be obtained in all languages...

283. Ji ber vê yekê, piştî van serpêhatiyên xwe, min xwe baş li bera xweş kir û biryar da ku ez li gora daxwaza dilê xwe bimeşim û mecal û îmkanên xwe li hemberî vî karê giran têxim kar.
283. Because of this, after these experiences of mine, I readied myself well and decided that I would walk according to the desire of my heart and put my means and possibilities to work against this heavy task.

284. Lê, asîman dûr, 'erd sext û asê bû.
284. But, the sky was far, the earth hard and steep.

285. Ez î pêşmergeyê gerok ê çiya û berriyan, bêcî û bêwar, herçar aliyên min rapêçayî, riyên bajar û avahiyan li ber min girtî, ez ê Şerefnameyê li ku bibînim, çawan bixwînim û wergerînim?
285. I, a roaming peshmerga of the mountains and plains, without a place or homeland, all four sides of me hemmed in, the roads of cities and buildings closed before me, where would I find the Şerefname, how would I read and translate it?

286. Ez ê kijan kitêbê wekî çavkanî
286. Which book would I as a source

287. bigrim berçav û ji bo muqayesekirina rast û şaşiyan li kijan nusxayê û li kîjan nivîsandinê binêrim?
287. consider and to which copy and which writing would I look to compare truths and errors?

288. Ez di vî halî de li derûdora xwe digeriyam, sitar û tebat bi min nedibû û min xwe dixwar.
288. In this state I would wander around my surroundings, I had no shelter and no rest and I was eating myself up (fretting).

289. Van hemû dane ciyekî, heta rê û rêbaran jî rê nedidan min, asteng û girêyan radixistin li ber min.
289. Putting all these in one place, even the roads and pathways gave me no way, they laid obstacles and knots before me.

290. Belê, ez demek di vi halî de mam û heta ez rastî Kek Salih Mehmûdê Barzanî hatim.
290. Yes, I remained in this state for a while and until I met Kek Salih Mehmûdê Barzanî.

291. Wî birayê binirx û hêja li wan berrî û deştan li min bû melek, Şerefnameya Farisî, wergerên Erebî yên M. 'Elî 'Ewnî Beg û Cemîl Rojbeyanî ji min re anîn û bi van diyariyên hêja ez ji wan girê û astengan rizgar kirim û wî derdê min ê giran çareser kir.
291. That valuable and worthy brother in those plains and steppes became an angel to me, he brought me the Persian Şerefname, the Arabic translations of M. 'Elî 'Ewnî Beg and Cemîl Rojbeyanî, and with these worthy gifts he saved me from those knots and obstacles and solved that heavy trouble of mine.

292. Min jî bêyî lam û cîm dest dan ber xwe û di pêdeçûna Şerefnameyê de bi ser ewqas tiştên seyr û 'ecêbmayî re derbas bûm ku, tu wextê ew tiştên ku ez tê de lê rast hatim, ber hiş û aqilê min re jî derbas nebûbûn.
292. And I without any lam or cim (without hesitation) set my hands to work and in going through the Şerefname I passed over so many strange and surprising things that, at no time had those things that I encountered in it ever even crossed my mind and intellect.

294. Wekî em dizanin, dema ku Îslamiyet di nav Kurdan de belav bû, gelek zana û nivîskarên mezin û birêz ji nav me peyda bûn û gelek nivîs di derheqê dîrok û edebiyata gelê xwe de nivîsandin.
294. As we know, when Islam spread among the Kurds, many great and honorable scholars and writers emerged from among us and wrote many texts concerning the history and literature of their people.

295. Lê tu wextê yek ji wan gelê xwe û eşîreta xwe dernexist pêş, pismam û kesên xwe bi bîra xwe neanî, xwe nêzîkî behs û bûyerên Kurd û Kurdistanê nekir.
295. But at no time did one of them bring their people and their tribe forward, did not recall their cousins and their own folk, did not bring themselves near to the accounts and events of the Kurds and Kurdistan.

296. Lê çi heye, ji hemû deman zêdetir di sedsala 11'an a koçî de, du kes ji nav me rabûne ku gunehê wan bi gelê wan ê lêqewimî hatiye û nan, av û himbêza gerim a Kurdan ji bîra xwe nebirine.
296. But what is there, more than all times in the 11th Hijri century, two persons rose from among us who pitied their stricken people and did not take the bread, water, and warm embrace of the Kurds out of their memory.

297. Bi nivîsandin, hawar û dadmendî Kurdan bi bîra xwe anîne û ew du kes jî: Şeref Xan û Ehmedê Xanî ne.
297. With writing, crying out and justice they remembered the Kurds and those two persons are: Şeref Xan and Ehmedê Xanî.

298. Tiştê seyr û ‘ecêb ev e ku; piştî van herdu dilsozan cardin ew derî hatine girtin û nêzîki 400 salan qifilkirî mane.
298. The strange and surprising thing is this: after these two loyal ones those doors were closed again and remained locked for nearly 400 years.

299. Car û caran hinek kesan xwestine vê jengara han paqij bikin.
299. From time to time some persons wanted to clean off this rust.

300. Navek re Hacî Qadir bangek derxistiye, lê kesekî xwe nêzîkî wî nekiriye, dema ku ew jî miriye, kesek din heta van nêzîkahiyên han xwe tixûbê vê meseleyê nekiriye.
300. With a name Hacî Qadir let out a cry, but nobody brought themselves near to him, and when he died too, nobody else until these recent times bounded themselves to this matter.

301. Di van dawiyan de jî ji Huseyin Huznî û Emîn Zekî Beg re li hev hatiye ku her yek ji wan li gor taqeta xwe têkoşîne ku berhemên me yên wendabûyî berhev bikin.
301. In these latter days as well it worked out for Huseyin Huznî and Emîn Zekî Beg that each of them according to their power struggled to compile our lost works.

302. Ji wan pey ve kesekî bi xwe re nedîtiye serî li vê meseleyê bide û serûberiyek rayxe meydanê.
302. After them no one has seen it in themselves to tackle this matter and lay out a proper arrangement in the arena.

303. Gelo di wê demê de çawan bûye ku bi ruhiyeta Kurdayetiyê bi taybetî di hundirê wan herdu kesan de ji hemû Kurdan bêtir gerr meşê kiriye?
303. I wonder how it was at that time that the spirit of Kurdishness specifically inside those two persons toured and walked more than all Kurds?

304. Min di derheqê Xanî de, di esnayê lêkolîna Mem û Zînê ya Mukriyanî de bîr û fikra xwe ya di vî warî de gotiye.
304. Regarding Xanî, during the research of Mem and Zîn of Mukriyanî I expressed my thought and opinion in this regard.

305. Şeref Xan jî ku roja ji dayîka xwe bûye şûn de, bi zanistiyê re rabûye.
305. And Şeref Xan, from the day he was born of his mother onward, was raised with knowledge.

306. Di seraya şahê Îranê de hatiye perwerdekirin, bi şehzadeyan re xwendiye,
306. He was educated in the palace of the Shah of Iran, he studied with princes,

307. wekî wan muamele dîtiye, beşdarê hemû huneran bûye, jêhatî û kêrhatî bûye.
307. he received treatment like them, he participated in all the arts, he became capable and useful.

308. Wekî bi xwe jî ji me re neqil dike, di mesele û bûyerên borî de û di hunera dîroknasî de gelek serkevtî bûye û ewqas hogirê wê babeta xwendewariyê bûye ku hemû dem û seetên xwe bi serpêhatiyên borî re derbas kiriye.
308. As he himself also conveys to us, he was very successful in the matters and events of the past and in the art of history, and he became so fond of that subject of literacy that he spent all his time and hours with past adventures.

309. Xwestiye ku di dîroknivîsî û dîroknasî de ciyekî bilind bigre û bi nav û deng be.
309. He wanted to take a high place in history-writing and historiography and be famous.

310. Ji ber vê li hemû deriyan xistiye û ji bo serkevtinê li ber wan deriyan sekiniye.
310. Because of this he knocked on all doors and stood before those doors for success.

311. Dîtiye ku çend kesên pêşiya wî di vê riyê re derbas bûne û gelek bi karmendî xebat kirine, lê tu mijarek peyda nekirine ku tê de bi tenê û bi serê xwe siwarê wê meydanê bin û bi kêfa dilê xwe hespê hunera xwe tê de bibezînin.
311. He saw that a few persons before him had passed through this road and had worked very professionally, but had not found any subject in which they alone and by themselves could be the rider of that arena and gallop the horse of their art in it to the joy of their heart.

312. Heta ji nişka ve melekê bext û talihê wî nêrînek xêrê bi ser de gerandiye ku ew mêrxasî, azayetî, merdî û başiya xwe bi bîr bîne.
312. Until suddenly the angel of his luck and fortune cast a glance of grace upon him, that he recall his bravery, gallantry, generosity, and goodness.

313. Wê çaxê gotiye ku; ez dikarim bi tîrekê çend nîşanan bidim.
313. At that time he said: I can hit several targets with one arrow.

314. 1. Tu kesekî destê xwe nedaye dîroka Kurdistanê, eger ew wê binivîse û derxe pêş, di vî karî de nav û dengê wî yê dahênerî belav dibe.
314. 1. No one has put their hand to the history of Kurdistan; if he writes it and brings it forward, his fame and renown of inventiveness will spread in this work.

315. 2. Navên navdarên Kurd wenda nabin, bi ber wê re jî navên bab û bapîrên wî jî ku Mîrên Bedlîsê ne bi jindarî bimînin û zarokên wan ên di pey wan de bên behsa şan û şohreta wê binemala mezin bixwînin.
315. 2. The names of the famous Kurds will not be lost, and along with that, the names of his fathers and grandfathers as well, who are the Mîrs of Bedlîs, will remain alive, and their children who come after them will read the account of the glory and fame of that great lineage.

316. 3. Bi xwe, her wekî di seranserê jiyana xwe de mîr û hukumdar e, bi vî karê han di rojên bên de jî di nav Kurdan de bi payedarî dimîne û li ser rûpelên rengîn ên nivîsan wî bi serbilindî bi bîr tînin û huner û kirinên xwe digihîne wan Kurdên rewşenbîr ên di pey wî de bên.
316. 3. He himself, just as he was a mîr and ruler throughout his life, with this work will remain with rank among the Kurds in the coming days as well, and they will remember him with pride on the colorful pages of writings, and he will deliver his art and deeds to those enlightened Kurds who come after him.

317. Têr û tesel bûye, hez nekiriye di muqabilê nivîsandinên wî de ji aliyê Dîwana Tirkan ve bexşişek jê re bê dan.
317. He was full and satisfied, he did not wish that in exchange for his writings a bounty be given to him by the Court of the Turks.

318. Tenê xwestiye Kurdan bi xwe û nivîskarên hevçerxên xwe bi hunermendiya xwe têbigihîne û ji vê pê ve jî çavên wî bi tiştekî zêde nebûye.
318. He only wanted to make the Kurds themselves and his contemporary writers understand his artistry, and beyond this his eyes were not set on much else.

319. Ev yeka han jî bi rastî jê re dest daye û jê re bûye alîkar û her bi xwe çawan xwestiye bi wî awayî bicî aniye.
319. This very thing truly lent him a hand and became his helper, and however he himself wanted it, he accomplished it in that way.

320. Dîroka Kurdan nivîsandiye û yên ku mirî bûne daye jiyandin, karek wisan kiriye ku tu wextê namire.
320. He wrote the history of the Kurds and gave life to those who were dead, he did a work such that it will never die.

321. Sipas ji Mîr Şeref Xanê mezin re ku tovên jiyanê di nav rûpelên Şerefnameyê de daye jiyandin.
321. Thanks to the great Mîr Şeref Xan who gave life to the seeds of life within the pages of the Şerefname.

322. Niha em dixwazin serîhildin, bijîn, bi eşkere û şanazî wî mîrê şêr û serfiraz bi bîr bînin û heta dinê ava ye jibîr nekin.
322. Now we want to rise up, to live, to openly and proudly remember that brave and triumphant mîr and not forget him as long as the world is built.

323. Ez dixwazim vê jî bêjim ku, dê gelê Kurd heta hetayê, çi dewlemend û çi feqîr hemû wext minnetar û sipasdarê wî bin.
323. I want to say this as well, that the Kurdish people forever, whether rich or poor, will at all times be indebted and thankful to him.

324. Şerefname, mêrxasname ye.
324. The Şerefname is a book of heroes.

325. Eger em hinek di nav rêz û rûpelên Şerefnameyê de dest bi gerê bikin, hûr hûr li ser bisekinin û lêbikolin, piraniya bûyerên tê de, ku dilkul û li berçav in, ya herî zêde diqqeta mirov dikêşe, mêrxasiya nedîtî ya gelê Kurd e.
325. If we somewhat begin to tour through the lines and pages of the Şerefname, dwell upon it and investigate minutely, the majority of the events in it, which are heart-wrenching and clear, what draws one's attention the most is the unseen bravery of the Kurdish people.

326. Ji serî heta biniya Şerefnameyê Kurdekî tirsonek nakeve berçav, hemû kesên mêrxas,
326. From the top to the bottom of the Şerefname not a single cowardly Kurd catches the eye, all are persons of bravery,

327. gernas û bêemsal in...
327. valiant and without equal...

328. Eger Mir Şeref Xan û Seydayê Xanî, tawanê yeknegirtin û ji hev dûrbûyîna me dikin situyê serhişki, guhgiranî û demarsituriya me, divê li min biborin, li gor baweriya min, perwerdekirina me ya bi hezaran sal em devvekirî hiştine û wisan bi me kiriye ku em duçarê çavlêgirtina fêlbaziya zalim û zordaran bibin.
328. If Mîr Şeref Xan and Seyda Xanî put the blame for our disunity and alienation from one another on the neck of our stubbornness, hard-of-hearing (stubborn ignorance) and thick-headedness, they must forgive me; according to my belief, our education of thousands of years has left us open-mouthed (gullible) and done to us such that we fall victim to turning a blind eye to the trickery of tyrants and oppressors.

329. Ew, ji bo têgihiştinî û nezaniya me qasê gir û çiyayên me yên asê û bilind û çemên me yên bornedayî bûne girê û asteng li pêşiya me.
329. Those, due to our lack of understanding and ignorance, as much as our steep and high hills and mountains and our un-fordable rivers, have become knots and obstacles before us.

330. Bi sedan car, heta bi hezaran car ji vê zêdetir jî em bi riya hîle, gotinên fêlbazî yên birayetî û dostiyê ve xapandine û bi van gotinan ewqas mejiyê me tijî kirine ku heta niha jî em dil, hejar û belengaz mane.
330. Hundreds of times, even thousands of times more than this, we have been deceived by means of tricks, tricky words of brotherhood and friendship, and with these words they filled our brains so much that even until now we have remained dejected, destitute and wretched.

331. Dema ku Sultan Murad gotiye: "Kurd, ji bilî yekîtiya li ser gotina Kelimeyê Şehadet pê ve, li ser tu tiştê yekîtiyek wan tune", ne mimkun e ku nezanîbe "xeta xwar di gayê pîr de ye", tiliya Sultan û yên bab û bapîrên wî jî di vê de heye.
331. When Sultan Murad said: "The Kurds, apart from unity upon the saying of the Word of Testimony (Shahada), have no unity on anything," it is impossible that he did not know "the crooked furrow is in the old ox" (the fault lies with the old ways), the finger of the Sultan and those of his fathers and grandfathers is in this as well.

332. Sultan Selim jî wiha gotiye: "Mêş, ji bo ku bikare têr rakeve, di nav kevlê însan de nerehetî derdixe".
332. Sultan Selim also said thus: "The fly, so that it can sleep fully, causes discomfort in the skin of humans."

333. Belê, wî zaniye ku kevlê xezê û kîsên lireyên elmasan Mamoste Mele Idris tê bîne rê û jê hêvî û lavayan bike: Tirkekî bike Mîrêmîranê Kurdistanê, wê çaxê dê Kurd jê re Qizilbaşan ji Kurdistanê derêxin.
333. Yes, he knew that fur cloaks and bags of diamond liras would bring the Teacher Mele Idris to line and make him hope and beg: Make a Turk the Mîrêmîran of Kurdistan, then the Kurds will drive the Qizilbash out of Kurdistan for him.

334. Ne dûr e, wê demê filoriyan kiribe nav kevlê xezê û berdabe bin cêba wî û gotibe: "Bila berîka min şêrîn bar be, para Kurdan ji jehr û mar be."
334. It is not far off (unlikely) that at that time he put florins (gold coins) into the fur cloak and dropped it under his pocket and said: "Let my pocket be sweetly loaded, let the Kurds' share be of poison and snake."

335. Ew Mele Idris ku hemû Kurdên hevdemên wî, wî mirovekî pîroz û payebilind zanîne, eger bi şîrê kolan nehatibaya perwerdekirin, qasê misqalek, qasê serê derziyê ruhê Kurdayetiyê tê de hebûya, dê Kurdan têbigihandiya ku: Dema hûn 'Eceman ji Kurdistanê derêxin, nebin xulam û nanxwerên Osmaniyan û werin bi xwe bibin xwediyê welatê xwe.
335. That Mele Idris whom all the Kurds of his time knew as a holy and high-ranking person, if he had not been raised with the milk of the streets, if there had been the weight of a mithqal, the size of a needle's head of the spirit of Kurdishness in him, he would have made the Kurds understand that: When you drive the Persians out of Kurdistan, do not become the servants and dependents of the Ottomans, but come and become the masters of your own country yourselves.

336. Eger xan ji xan re û beg ji beg re serî natewînin, bila ez bibim hukumdar û rîsipiyê we û bi karê xwe ve rabim, mamosteyekî bi nav û deng ê Kurdistanê ji dergevanekî Tirkan û biyaniyan baştir e.
336. If a khan does not bow his head to a khan and a beg to a beg, let me be your ruler and elder and rise to my work; a famous teacher of Kurdistan is better than a gatekeeper of the Turks and foreigners.

337. Lê, wî wiha nekiriye, heta ku em bêjin ka gelo dê bikira?
337. But, he did not do so, to the point that we might say, would he ever have done it?

338. Çîrokek bi vî awayî 'ecêb û seyr jî di derheqê Kerîm Xanê Zend de heye.
338. A similarly strange and surprising story also exists regarding Kerim Khan Zand.

339. Li gor vi fermanrewayê desthilatdar û mezin ê Îranê, lênayê ku navê Şahîtiyê bi Kurdeki ve hebe.
339. According to this powerful and great ruler of Iran, it is not fitting that the title of Kingship be attached to a Kurd.

340. Ji ber vê jî ji xwe re gotiye: "Wekîl El-Re'ya" (Berdevkê Miskinan)
340. And because of this he called himself: "Wakil Al-Ra'aya" (The Representative of the Subjects).

341. Mele Xetî, alîkarvanê mîrê Rewandizê bûye û li milê jorê Fermanrewayên Kurdistanê rûniştiye.
341. Mele Xetî was the assistant of the mîr of Rewandiz and sat at the upper side of the Rulers of Kurdistan.

342. Lê, ji bo ku nanê mîr nanê cî bûye, li xweşa mela neçûye.
342. But, because the mîr's bread was local bread, it did not please the mullah.

343. Ew hînê maaş û nanê biyaniyan bûye.
343. He was accustomed to the salary and bread of foreigners.

344. Bi fetwayek
344. With a fatwa

345. dewleteke Kurdî ya serbixwe sernixom dike û heta xwe nake nanxwerê dagirkeran sebr û tebata wî nayê.
345. he overthrows an independent Kurdish state and until he makes himself the bread-eater (dependent) of the occupiers, he has no patience or rest.

346. Em qet dûr neçin, her di dema me bi xwe de, di nîvê duduyan a çerxa bîstan de, gelek xwendewarên di nav Kurdîziman de, di çarçeveya Kurdistana niştiman de bûn asteng û kusp li pêşiya Kurdên azadîxwaz û ji bo ji holêrakirina navê Kurdistanê yê pîroz bi dil û can têdikoşîn.
346. Let us not go far at all, right in our own time, in the second half of the twentieth century, many educated people among the Kurdish-speakers, within the framework of the homeland Kurdistan, became obstacles and hurdles before the freedom-seeking Kurds and for the eradication of the sacred name of Kurdistan struggled with heart and soul.

347. Min bi çavê serê xwe dîtin, di şerê xwînavî yê Barzanî de, ku bi hezaran lawên Kurd ên nexwenda, hejar û derbeder ruhê wan li ser destê wan û destê wan jî li ser xenceran li dijî dagirkerê regezperest û bêmerhemet serî hildidan, ew xwendewarên me di pêşiya leşkerên dijmin û çek li milê wan dihatin gundên welatê xwe Kurdistanê dişewitandin, Kurd dikuştin, bax û baxçeyên bêçareyan kokbir dikirin, malên Kurdan talan dikirin, zarokên sava yên dergûşan de dikirin nav girên agirên sor û piştre rê nîşanê firokên dijmin dikirin ku çawan bombeyan bi ser wan de birijînin, çawan ew bêdeng û bêhîs bikin... Ji bo çi?
347. I saw with my own eyes, in the bloody war of Barzani, where thousands of uneducated, poor and displaced Kurdish sons, their souls on their hands and their hands on their daggers, were rising up against the racist and merciless occupier, those educated people of ours in front of the enemy's soldiers and with weapons on their shoulders were coming and burning the villages of their country Kurdistan, killing Kurds, uprooting the orchards and gardens of the helpless, plundering the homes of Kurds, putting infant children in cradles into the hills of red fire and then pointing the way for the enemy's planes on how to pour bombs on them, how to make them silent and senseless... For what?

348. Çunkî ew aîleya ku ew di nav de hatine perwerdekirin, nezanîne ji bilî bindestî û biyanîperestiyê pê ve rewş û jiyanek din jî heye.
348. Because that family in which they were raised, did not know that there is any condition and life other than subjugation and worshipping foreigners.

349. Di dibistanan de jî her eynê van tiştan hîn bûne.
349. In schools as well they learned exactly these very things.

350. Li gor baweriya min, gunehê hînbûna me ya ji bo xulamtiyê û xwe kêmzanîna xwe, maka hemû derd û belayên me ne.
350. According to my belief, the sin of our conditioning for servitude and considering ourselves inferior, is the mother of all our troubles and calamities.

351. Çi çaxê bîr û baweriya piraniya Kurdan bigihîje wê qonaxê ku bêjin mafê wan ê jiyanê yê wekî hemû xelkên din heye, ew ji tu kesê kêmtir nîn in û koletî tiştekî şerm û sernizmî ye, wê çaxê ne xayînên me yên xwendewar, ne cehşên me yên nexwendewar û ne jî yên wekî Mele Xetî yên ji rê derketî di nav me de peyda dibin.
351. Whenever the thought and belief of the majority of Kurds reaches that stage that they say they have a right to life like all other peoples, they are not lesser than anyone and slavery is a shameful and degrading thing, at that time neither our educated traitors, nor our uneducated donkeys (collaborators), nor those derailed ones like Mele Xetî will emerge among us.

352. Carekê, mirovekî Mukriyanî yê navê wî Hemed Axa, ji xulamekî xwe yê kal û emekdar re dibêje: "Tu mirovekî rîspî yî, ez şerm dikim tu xwarinên bermayên min bixwî.
352. Once, a Mukriyanî man whose name was Hemed Axa, says to an old and loyal servant of his: "You are a white-bearded (elder) man, I am ashamed that you eat my leftover foods.

353. Li dîwanxaneyê rûne û bila sîniya xwarina te jî bi ya min re bînin."
353. Sit in the guesthouse and let them bring your food tray along with mine."

354. Mam Xidirê xulam ji axa re dibêje: "Axa, ez ji te hêvî û rîca dikim, ez ê şanzdeh teneke rûn bidim te, lê tu jiyana min a ewqas salan ku ez hîn bûme neguherînî.
354. Uncle Xidir the servant says to the axa: "Axa, I hope and beg of you, I will give you sixteen tins of butter, just do not change my life of so many years to which I have become accustomed.

355. Eger tu tiştekî wisan bikî, ez naçar im ku bar bikim û biçim û li ber deriyê axayekî din bijîm.
355. If you do such a thing, I will be forced to pack up and go and live at the door of another axa.

356. Wer e, piştî van salên min şûn de destê min ji xwarinên min ên bermayî neke"
356. Come, after these years of mine do not separate my hand from my leftover foods."

357. Qasê ku ez dizanim, ev çîroka han çîroka hemû Kurdan e, heta xulamtî û bermayîxwarin neyê guhertin, nabe bibe rûniştiyê dîwanxaneyê û guhertina wê jî karekî zehmet e.
357. As far as I know, this story is the story of all Kurds; until servitude and eating leftovers is changed, one cannot become a sitter of the guesthouse, and its changing is also a difficult task.

358. Dema ku ev yek biguhere, bêşik jiyan jî dê ber bi başiyê ve biguhere.
358. When this changes, undoubtedly life as well will change towards goodness.

359. ## ŞEREFNAME HUNERNAME YE
359. ## ŞEREFNAME IS A BOOK OF ART

360. Ew kesên şarezayê edebiyata Farisî ya nû û kevn in, piştî vê eserê dixwînin û li ser diaxivin, di netîceyê de ji wan re ronî dibe ku di dema Sefewiyan de di Îranê de -ku Mîr Şeref Xan di wê demê de jiya ye- edebiyata Faris, ketiye nav rengek taybetî û cilûbergek fiş, sik û nexweş lê bûye.
360. Those persons who are experts in the new and old Persian literature, after they read this work and talk about it, in the result it becomes clear to them that in the time of the Safavids in Iran -where Mîr Şeref Xan lived in that time- Persian literature fell into a specific color and put on an ostentatious, ugly and unpleasant attire.

361. Gotin û rêzên ne pêwîst ên hìşk û req ên derwêşane ketine nav û ketiye nav rewş û haleke pejmûrde.
361. Unnecessary, dry and rigid dervish-like words and lines fell into it and it fell into a withered state and condition.

362. Fars bi xwe jî wê demê meydana şer a herî nexweş a edebiyata xwe hesêb dikin.
362. The Persians themselves as well consider that time the most unpleasant battlefield of their literature.

363. Li gor wan, edebiyata wan ji geşekirinê sekiniye, hetta em vê daynin milekî, ber bi çilmisînê ve çûye.
363. According to them, their literature stopped growing, even leaving this aside, it went towards fading.

364. Eger em wan nivîsandinên Farisî yên wê çaxê hatine nivîsandin bi Farisiya Şerefnameyê re muqayese bikin, tu şik tê de tune ku Şerefname serkopa hemû nivîsandinên wê çaxê yên bi Farisî hatine nivîsandi ne.
364. If we compare those Persian writings that were written at that time with the Persian of the Şerefname, there is no doubt in it that the Şerefname is the crown of all writings of that time which were written in Persian.

365. Materyalên ziman ên ji bo nivîsandina Şerefnameyê hatine bikaranîn, ji xulaseyên zimanê Farisî yê wê çaxê hatine helbijartin û nimûneyên edebiyata Farisî ya wê serdemê nîşanê xelkê dinê daye.
365. The language materials that were used for writing the Şerefname, were selected from the essence of the Persian language of that time and showed the world the examples of the Persian literature of that era.

366. Wekî me jî got, wî xwestiye di nivîsandina dîrokê de jî karekî wisan bike ku ji hemû dîroknivîsên hevçerx bibore û kesek bi xwe re nebîne ku dev bavêje wî.
366. As we also said, he wanted in the writing of history as well to do such a work that he surpasses all contemporary historians and no one would see it in themselves to throw words at him (criticize him).

367. Yanê bi tîrekî li du armancan xistiye.
367. That is, with one arrow he hit two targets.

368. Yekem, dîroka Kurdan nivîsandiye û ya duyem jî di edebiyata Farisî de jî şaranek mîmarî û cewherek sipehî vehunandiye.
368. First, he wrote the history of the Kurds, and second, in Persian literature as well he wove an architectural structure and a beautiful gem.

369. Min Hejarî feqîr jî xwest bi wergerandina karê wî fermanrewayê şêresiwarê bi nav û deng, bi berekî li du armancan bidim: Yekem, bûyerên dîrokî wekî di Şerefnameyê de hene bi zimanê Kurdî pêşkêş bikim.
369. I, the poor Hejar, also wanted by translating the work of that famous lion-riding ruler, to hit two targets with one stone: First, to present the historical events as they are in the Şerefname in the Kurdish language.

370. Duyem jî, hewl bidim ku destkevtiyên edebiyata Kurdî yên îro tê de bicî bikim.
370. Second as well, to strive to embed the achievements of today's Kurdish literature within it.

371. Vêca ger min karî ez van cîbicî bikim, ji bo hejarekî wekî min serbilindî û rûspîtî ye.
371. So if I have managed to implement these, for a poor one like me it is a pride and honor.

372. Qet nebe bi vî karî qevdek gul ji edebiyata Kurdî re tê diyarîkirin.
372. At the very least, with this work a handful of roses is presented as a gift to Kurdish literature.

373. ## MIN ŞEREFNAME ÇAWAN WERGERAND
373. ## HOW I TRANSLATED THE ŞEREFNAME

374. Wergera Xeyam di destê min de bû.
374. The translation of Khayyam was in my hand.

375. Mamostekî bi qedir û bi qîmet ji min pirsî: "Te filan bêjeya Farisî bi Kurdî çawan bikar aniye?"
375. An honorable and valuable teacher asked me: "How did you use such-and-such Persian word in Kurdish?"

376. Min jê re got; ezbenî, daxwaza min ew nîn e ku ez Farisî hînê Kurdan bikim.
376. I said to him; my lord, my desire is not to teach Persian to the Kurds.

377. Ez tenê bîr û baweriya Xeyam pêşkêşî Kurdan dikim.
377. I am only presenting the thought and belief of Khayyam to the Kurds.

378. Di wergera Şerefnameyê de jî ez her li ser vê riya han meşiyam.
378. In the translation of the Şerefname as well I continued to walk on this very path.

379. Min kurtiya bûyerên dîrokê bi Kurdî raxistiye ber destê Kurdan.
379. I have laid out the essence of the historical events in Kurdish into the hands of the Kurds.

380. Di wan ciyên ku Şeref Xan ji bo xweşkirin û xemlandina bendan û raxistina meydana
380. In those places where Şeref Xan, for the beautification and decoration of the stanzas and the laying out of the arena

381. Şeran li ber çav û methên mîr û mezinan, bi edîbane bi Farisî dest hildaye û xwe pê aciz kiriye.
381. of battles before the eyes and the praises of mîrs and elders, raised his hand in a literary manner in Persian and troubled himself with it.

411. Ew kesên wijdana xwe bikin rehberê xwe û bi awakî hûr û kûr rojên borî yên gelê Kurd bikolin, gelek baş têdigihin ku ev gel di hemû deman de -pêşiya Islamiyetê û piştî Islamiyetê- dîroka wê tijî ji serbilindiyan e û di karên baş û merdane de destekî wî yê bilind jî heye.
411. Those persons who make their conscience their guide and minutely and deeply investigate the past days of the Kurdish people, will understand very well that this people in all times -before Islam and after Islam- its history is full of glories, and in good and manly deeds it has a high hand as well.

412. Di xurtkirina bingehê Islamiyetê û bilindkirina koşka rewşenbiriya 'Ereb de, bêtexsîratî beşdarî kiriye.
412. In strengthening the foundation of Islam and raising the palace of Arab intellectuality, it has participated unsparingly.

413. Tu li kijan koşeyê û li kijan deverê hunera Îslamiyetê bigerî, destkariya Kurdan tê de hebûye û di warê nexş û nîgarên cure cure de xebat kiriye.
413. In whichever corner and whichever region of the art of Islam you search, the handiwork of the Kurds has been in it, and in the field of various patterns and designs it has worked.

414. Lê dîroknivîsên vê rojhilata han her di destpêkî de, bi taybetî di van sedsalên dawiyê de, bîra gelperestiya xwe kirine û hemû edîbên li ser regezên din ji xwe re henek hesibandine, tu wextê pêwist nezanîne li Kurdên hevdîn û hevdemên xwe binêrin û bên hawara wan.
414. But the historians of this Orient right from the beginning, especially in these last centuries, remembered their own chauvinism and considered all men of letters from other races as a joke to themselves, they never found it necessary to look at the Kurds of the same religion and their contemporaries and come to their aid.

415. Gelek zanayên jîr û şairên Kurdan jî bi zimanê Erebî, Farisî, Tirkî, Fransizî û İngilîzî gelek kitêb nivîsandine, lê tu carê hiş û aqilê wan neketiye ku bîra xwe û zimanê xwe jî bikin û nêrîn û çavekî bi ser regez û qewmê xwe de jî bigerînin.
415. Many smart scholars and poets of the Kurds have also written many books in the Arabic, Persian, Turkish, French and English languages, but never did it cross their mind and intellect to remember themselves and their own language as well and to cast a glance and an eye over their own race and people too.

416. Êdî di çarçeveya vê terkexemiyê de Kurdan her çi tiştê ji bo İslamiyetê kirin û çi serbilindiyên wan li ser rûpelên dîrokê hebûn, şik jê hatin kirin û ev kirinên han hem ji bîra Kurdan û hem ji yên biyaniyan çûn.
416. Now within the framework of this abandonment, whatever things the Kurds did for Islam and whatever glories they had on the pages of history, doubts were cast upon them and these deeds were forgotten both by the Kurds and by the foreigners.

417. Zimanê şêrîn û rewa yê Kurd jî di piraniya bajarên mezin ên Kurdistanê yên wekî Mûsil, Diyarbekir, Sine û Mêrdînê
417. The sweet and fluent language of the Kurds as well, in the majority of the big cities of Kurdistan such as Mosul, Diyarbekir, Sine and Mardin,

418. de berê xwe da ber jarî û qelsiyê, zimanên biyanî bi awakî wisan li wan deran cî bûn ku, eger rêwiyek neşereza û nezîrek li wan ciyan re derbas be û lê binêre, dikare bêje ku tu peywendiyek van ciyan bi Kurdistanê ve nemaye.
418. turned towards weakness and frailty, foreign languages settled in those places in such a way that, if an unknowledgeable and unsharp traveler passes by those places and looks at it, he might say that no connection of these places with Kurdistan remains.

419. Along with this too, the vast majority of the residents of these big cities, by blood, by language, and by their customs and traditions, are good Kurds.

420. Lê wisan lê hatiye kirin, wexte Erebî, Tirkî û Farisî zimanê wan dadibeline.
420. But it has been done in such a way, at times Arabic, Turkish and Persian swallow their language.

421. In many places they only call villagers and nomads Kurds, the people who are residents of the cities are not considered Kurds.

422. Vêca dema ku min ew dîtin, dilê min serî hilda û min ji xwe re got; ez ê li pey coxrafya û dîroka Kurd û Kurdistanê bikevim, çi koşe û kuncikên kitêbxaneyên Qahîre û Asîtaneyê (Stenbol) hene ez ê bigerim û ew destnivîsên behsa Kurdan dikin, toza li ser wan bimalim, wan bidest bixim û têxim ber destan.
422. 610. There is no doubt or obstacle in the scientific value of this Kurdish history.

611. Li gel bi ser de derbazbûna 300 salan*, hê di Rojhilatê de karekî bi vî awayî nehatiye kirin ku bi Şerefnameyê re bê muqayesekirin.
611. Along with the passing of 300 years over it*, still in the East a work in this manner has not been done that can be compared with the Şerefname.

612. Bi rastî jî, ji bilî Şerefnameyê, di derheqê Kurdan de çend pîneyên li ha û li wir û çend perçeyên kêm nebin, ku ketine nav kitêban, di vî warî de ji vê çêtir berhem bi dest nakevin.
612. Truly too, besides the Şerefname, regarding the Kurds save for a few patches here and there and a few small pieces, which have fallen into books, in this field works better than this cannot be obtained.

613. Ew çend perçe jî her çiqas zêde jî bin, lê tiştekî wisan nadin dest ku mirov pê bêdeng be.
613. Those few pieces too no matter how many they are, yet they do not give to hand something such that one can be silent by it (be satisfied).

614. Em bi wan nikarin bigihin avakirina dîrokekî ji gelê Kurd re, ku gelek şax jê derdikevin û her şaxek xwediyê dîroka xwe ya serbixwe ye.
614. We cannot with them reach the construction of a history for the Kurdish people, from which many branches emerge and each branch is the owner of its independent history.

615. Şeref, ji bili wan bûyerên ji dîroka Erebî, Farisî û Tirkî berhev kirine, beşek zêde jî ji serpêhatiyên nû û girîng ên wisan xistiye ser ku, bi karekî zêde eşîretan bi rêk û pêkî bi hev ve girêdaye û bi vî karî valatiyên gelek zêde yên dîroka gelên Asyayê tijî kiriye.
615. Şeref, besides those events he compiled from Arabic, Persian and Turkish history, added a large portion as well of new and important adventures such that, with great work he tied the tribes together orderly and properly and with this work filled very many voids of the history of the peoples of Asia.

616. Di vir de jî başî û qenciyek gelek mezin kiriye.
616. In this too he did a very great goodness and favor.

617. Di derheqê coxrafya, karekter û rewşa wan de xwediyê zanyarî bûye, tiştên wan ên baş û qenc helbijartiye û di dîroka xwe de bicî kiriye.
617. Regarding their geography, character, and condition he became the owner of knowledge, selected their good and favorable things, and placed them in his history.

618. Bi vê ji bihayê edebî ya nivîsandina xwe gelek bilind kiriye.
618. With this too he raised the literary price of his writing greatly.

619. Ew rexneyên ku em bikarin li Şeref bikin, ev herdu xalên han in: Yek ji wan; awayê nivîsandina kitêbê ye û ya din jî awayê rêzkirina wê ye.
619. Those criticisms that we can make of Şeref, are these two points: One of them; is the manner of writing of the book and the other too is its manner of arrangement.

620. Dibû hemû bûyerên dîroka Kurd bikira du beş û beşek ji ya din gelek cihêtir bûya.
620. He should have made all the events of Kurdish history into two parts and one part would be very different from the other.

621. Ew milên ku gelek diqqet dikişînin, ew in: Ji bo ku di pêş de tiştek nehatiye nivîsandin, dîroka wan a borî ji Kurdan seh kiriye, hinek jî bi çavên xwe dîtine û bi xwe bûne şahid.
621. Those aspects which draw much attention, are these: Because previously nothing was written, he heard their past history from the Kurds, some as well he saw with his own eyes and became a witness himself.

622. Kitêb, di vî warî de çavkaniyeke wisan e ku, tu wextê ziwa nabe û di vê kitêbê de ew çavkanî ji hişkbûnê hatine rizgarkirin.
622. The book, in this regard is such a source that, it never dries up, and in this book those sources were saved from drying out.

623. Lê yên ji kitêbên din wergirtine, di wergirtina bûyeran de gelek hişê xwe nedaye ser û xwe ji şaşiyan neparastiye.
623. But those he took from other books, in the taking of events he did not put his mind to it much and did not protect himself from errors.

624. Hinek caran di wergirtina bûyeran de ketiye nav aloziyê, navên wan şaş nivîsandiye û rastî bêserûberiyek wisan kiriye ku mirov nikare çavê xwe lê bigre.
624. Sometimes in taking the events he fell into confusion, wrote their names erroneously, and in truth made such a mess that one cannot close one's eyes to it.

625. Di warê awayê nivîsandinê de jî, eger ew şi'irên hejmarên wan zêde û ew çend bûyerên hêlm û sawmgiran yên kêm nebin, ku Rojhilatî jê hez dikin û li gor me ji ne baş e, nivîsa wî bi giştî hêsan û rewan e.
625. In the field of the manner of writing as well, if it were not for those poems whose numbers are many and those few steam-filled (pompous) and heavy-dreaded events, which Orientals love and according to us too is not good, his writing generally is easy and fluent.

626. Tê de çend bûyerên ji rastiyê dûr jî li ber çav dikevin.
626. In it a few events far from reality also catch the eye.

627. Di çapê de, min nişana pirsê li ber wan gotinên ku ez li ser wan dudil bûm dani.
627. In the print, I placed a question mark before those words upon which I was hesitant.

628. Di dawiya kitêbê de ez bi dirêjî li ser wan xalên ku min îşareta pirsê li ber daniye disekinim, behsa wan dikim û ew "nivîsandinên xwe" dihesibînim.
628. At the end of the book I dwell at length upon those points before which I placed the question mark, I discuss them and I consider them "my own writings".

629. Li gor tarixa nivisandina vê pêşgotinê 300 sal e. (Wergêr)
629. According to the date of writing of this preface it is 300 years. (Translator)

630. Li gel van tevan jî, nabe rexneyên min girîng bên girtin, çunkî ne tenê Şeref Xan, hemû nivîskarên Rojhilatî guhên xwe nadin van cure awayên han ku dibê mirov di lêkolînên zanyarî de lê hişyar be.
630. Along with all these too, my criticisms cannot be taken as important, because not only Şeref Xan, all Oriental writers do not give their ears to these types of methods that one must be awake to in scientific researches.

631. Li gor bîr û baweriya piraniya Rojhilatiyan, ev awa dîroknivîs baştir e.
631. According to the thought and belief of the majority of Orientals, this manner of history-writing is better.

632. Di awayî warê rêzkirinê de jî dibê mirov ji yekî wekî Şeref Xan re çavên xwe bigre.
632. In the manner of the field of arrangement as well one must close one's eyes for someone like Şeref Xan.

633. Çunki, piraniya jiyana xwe di fermana dîwan, di şer û sergêjiyan de derbas kiriye.
633. Because, he spent the majority of his life in the command of the court, in war, and in confusions.

634. Şerefname, di welatên Rojhilatê de gelek kêm belav bûye û sebebê wê jî gelek hêsan e:
634. The Şerefname, spread very little in the countries of the East and its reason as well is very simple:

635. Roleke girîng a gelê Kurd di Asyayê de tunebûye û dîroka wan jî ji bo xelkê Rojhilatê gelek nehatiye bikaranîn.
635. An important role of the Kurdish people in Asia did not exist and their history as well was not much used for the people of the East.

636. Lê cardin, bi ser vê de jî, di ferhenga huner û kitêban de, ku Hacî Xelîfe (Katib Çelebî) nivîsandiye, ji navê Şerefnameyê re ciyek baş amade kiriye. (Binêre: Keşfu'l-Zunûn, çapa Filorchil(?), hejmar: 2135-2144).
636. But again, on top of this too, in the dictionary of art and books, which Hacı Halife (Katip Çelebi) wrote, he prepared a good place for the name of the Şerefname. (See: Kashf al-Zunun, Fluegel(?) edition, number: 2135-2144).

637. Wî nivîskarê mezin, di derheqê coxrafyayê de kitêba Cîhannûma nivîsandiye.
637. That great author, wrote the book Cihannüma regarding geography.

638. Her wiha eger em Zeynelabidîn yê ku kitêba Riyadu'l-Seyyahe nivîsandiye jî bihesibînin, van herdukan jî di derheqê Kurdan de gelek tişt ji Şerefnameyê wergirti- ne.
638. Likewise if we consider Zeynelabidin who wrote the book Riyad al-Siyahah as well, these two too took many things regarding the Kurds from the Şerefname.

639. Di Asyayê de du caran jî wergera Şerefnameyê bi zimanê Tirkî hatiye kirin.
639. In Asia as well the translation of the Şerefname was done twice in the Turkish language.

640. Yek ji wan, yekê navê wî Samî bûye û ji bo mîrekî Kurd ê navê wî Mistefa Beg wergerandiye (Binêre: Murlî, Fihrista Destnivîsên Erebî û Farisî yên kevin ku di Kitêbxaneya Asya ya Qraltî ya Brîtanya Mezin û Irlandayê de heye).
640. One of them, was someone whose name was Samî and he translated it for a Kurdish mîr whose name was Mistefa Beg (See: Morley, Descriptive Catalogue of the Historical Manuscripts in the Arabic and Persian Languages preserved in the Library of the Royal Asiatic Society of Great Britain and Ireland).

641. Hacî Xelîfe, kitêb bi navê “Tarîxa Şeref Xanê Bedlîsî" bi nav kiriye.
641. Hacı Halife, named the book with the name "The History of Şeref Xanê Bedlîsî".

642. Di Ewrûpayê de cara yekem, Hêrbilo ev behsa han vekiriye û di du maqaleyên ku li ser kitêbxaneyên Rojhilat nivîsandine, behsa wê kiriye.
642. In Europe for the first time, d'Herbelot opened this discussion and in two articles which he wrote on the libraries of the East, discussed it.

643. Carekê jê re dibêje: “Dîroka Şeref Xanê Bedlîsî" û careke din jî bi navê "Dîroka Kurdan" binav kiriye. (Binêre: Mr. Kut)
643. Once he calls it: "The History of Şeref Xanê Bedlîsî" and another time as well he named it with the name "The History of the Kurds". (See: Mr. Quatremère)

644. Ev herdu maqaleyên han jî ne kêm û ne zêde ji ferhenga Hacî Xelîfe hatine wergirtin.
644. These two articles as well were neither less nor more taken from the dictionary of Hacı Halife.

645. Wisan dixuyê J. Malcolm li pêşiya hemû Ewrûpiyan nusxayek Şerefnameyê bi dest xistiye, ew jî ji aliyê serokê Eşîreta Muheyzî (Mehîzî, Mihzî) ya kurd jê re hatiye dan.
645. It appears thus that J. Malcolm before all Europeans obtained a copy of the Şerefname; that too was given to him by the leader of the Kurdish Muheyzi (Mehizi, Mihzi) Tribe.

646. Li ser nivîsandiye: "Kurdîn Kurunik” hê “Fun Şeref”.
646. On it is written: "Kurdîn Kurunik" or "Fun Şeref".

647. Ev nusxeya han di Kitêbxaneya Asya ya Qraltî ya Brîtanya Mezin û Irlandayê de ye. (Binêre: Fihrist)
647. This copy is in the Library of the Royal Asiatic Society of Great Britain and Ireland. (See: Index)

648. Gelek jî ecêb e ku, Tarîxa Fermanrewayên Erdelan jî behs kiriye.
648. It is also very strange that, he discussed the History of the Rulers of Ardalan as well.

649. Te de hatiye gotin ku di sala 1225'ê koçî (1810 zayînî) de hatiye nivîsandin. (Binêre: Kataloga Murlî û Morilo).
649. In it, it is said that it was written in the Hijri year 1225 (1810 Gregorian). (See: Catalogue of Morley and Morillo).

650. J. Malcolm, di dîroka xwe ya Farisî de, ku di sala 1815'an de weşandiye, gelek caran behsa Şerefnameyê dike û hetta çend kurtebû yer
650. J. Malcolm, in his Persian history, which he published in the year 1815, discusses the Şerefname many times and even several short events

651. jî jê werdigre.
651. he takes from it as well.

652. Lê, gelek bi giringi negirtiye û bi dirêjahî li ser naveroka wê nesekiniye.
652. But, he did not take it with much importance and did not dwell at length upon its content.

653. Em dikarin bêjin ku heta sala 1826'an, Ewrûpiyan qîmeta Şerefnameyê nezanîne.
653. We can say that until the year 1826, Europeans did not know the value of the Şerefname.

654. Çunki, diqqeta rewşenbîrên wê nekêşaye.
654. Because, it did not draw the attention of its intellectuals.

655. Ez li vir bi xwe re dibînim vê rastiya han raxim ber çavan ku, di saya zanayên Rûsî de ev diyariya hêja pêşkêşî zanistiyê bûye (Binêre: Durn, Mekoyî Asyayî, beşa duduyan).
655. I see it with myself here to lay out this very truth before the eyes that, in the shadow (thanks to) of Russian scholars this worthy gift has been presented to science (See: Dorn, Asiatic Museum, second part).

656. Mr. Frach, kesê yekem bû ku di sala 1826'an de, bangek ji bo vê dîroka Kurdan derxist û kurtelêkolîn û şilovekirinek li ser nivîsand û di dawiyê de daxwaz kir ku ev dîroka girîng a Asyayê bê wergerandin.
656. Mr. Fraehn, was the first person who in the year 1826, issued a call for this history of the Kurds and wrote a short research and analysis on it and in the end requested that this important history of Asia be translated.

657. Ew wiha dibêje: "Wergerandina vê afirandina bi qîmet dê bibe pencereyek û dîroka Asyayê ji me re ronî bike."
657. He says thus: "The translation of this valuable creation will become a window and illuminate the history of Asia for us."

658. Di eynê salê de, Mr. Volkov, bîranînek li ser dîroka Fars belav kiriye û tê de behsa navê Şerefnameyê û hinek jî ya Şeref Xan bi xwe kiriye.
658. In the same year, Mr. Volkov, distributed a memoir on the history of the Persians and in it discussed the name of the Şerefname and some of Şeref Xan himself as well.

659. (Rojnameya Asya, beş: 8, sal: 1826, rûpel: 291-298)
659. (Journal Asiatique, part: 8, year: 1826, pages: 291-298)

660. Qasek piştî wî jî, F. B. Charmoy ku li St. Petersburgê mamosteyê zimanê Rojhilat bû, li ser pêşniyara Mr. Frach dest bi wergera Şerefnameyê kir (Binêre: Frahc, Jiyana Şêx Sefi Erdewîlî), eger nexweşî nebûya, wî Rojhilatnasê hêja dê xebatek wisan bixistaya ber dest ku diqqeta hemû zanayan bikêşaya.
660. A while after him as well, F. B. Charmoy who in St. Petersburg was a teacher of the Oriental language, upon the suggestion of Mr. Fraehn started the translation of the Şerefname (See: Fraehn, The Life of Şêx Sefî Erdewîlî); if there had not been illness, that worthy Orientalist would have placed such a work at hand that it would have drawn the attention of all scholars.

661. Di dema me de jî, ku di saya têkoşîna zana û gerokan, lêkolîn li ser ziman, dîrok û coxrafya Kurdistanê di halekî gelek geşesitandinî de ye, Şerefname, ji bo vê mebesta taybetî gelek bi kêr û bi feyde ye û hejmara kesên jê îstîfade dikin, roj bi roj zêde dibe.
661. In our time as well, when in the shadow (thanks to) the struggle of scholars and travelers, research on the language, history and geography of Kurdistan is in a very developing state, the Şerefname, for this specific purpose is very useful and beneficial, and the number of people utilizing it, increases day by day.

662. Mr. Etienne Marc Quatremère, di Dîroka Moxol û Fars a çapa Parîsê û di rûpela 319-329'an de, çend perçe ji Şerefnameyê wergirtiye.
662. Mr. Etienne Marc Quatremère, in The History of the Mongols and Persians of the Paris edition and on pages 319-329, took several pieces from the Şerefname.

663. Eger em li fihrista "Destnivîsên Rojhilat Di Kitêbxaneya Qeyser de", St. Petersburg, sal: 1852, rûpel: 295'an binêrin, bi serê xwe maqaleyek ji vê dîrokê re amade kiriye.
663. If we look at the index "Oriental Manuscripts In the Imperial Library", St. Petersburg, year: 1852, page: 295, he prepared an article by itself for this history.

664. Dr. Barb, ku di derheqê felsefeya dîrokê de nivîsandiye û behsa Tirkiyê û Fars dike, di du maqaleyan de kurtiya Şerefnameyê ji serî heta binî bicî kiriye û li ser sekiniye.
664. Dr. Barb, who wrote regarding the philosophy of history and discusses Turkey and Persia, in two articles placed the summary of the Şerefname from top to bottom and dwelt upon it.

665. Her wiha wî nivîskarî maqaleya sisiyan li ser pênc binemalên Kurd nivîsandiye ku mirov dikare bêje, beşa yekê pê temam kiriye.
665. Likewise that author wrote a third article on five Kurdish families which one can say, he completed the first part with it.

666. Mr. Morey jî di fihrista Şerefnameyê de navê wan eşîret û serokên bi şaşî hatine nivîsandin, daye ber du wergerên Tirkî û ew rast kirine, ku ev herdu wergerên han di Muzexaneya Brîtanyayê de ne.
666. Mr. Morey as well in the index of the Şerefname put the names of those tribes and leaders which had been erroneously written, against two Turkish translations and corrected them, which these two very translations are in the British Museum.

667. Mr. Larsen, di behsa Kurdan de kurtemaqaleyek wî li ser dîroka Kurdan heye û çend bûyerên 'ecêb neqil dike ku ji Şerefnameyê wergirtiye.
667. Mr. Larsen, in the discussion of the Kurds has a short article of his on the history of the Kurds and narrates several strange events which he took from the Şerefname.

668. Lê di dawiyê de Mr. Kotik rastiya wê gotiye û di vî warî de nivîs
668. But in the end Mr. Kotik told its truth and in this field writings

669. weşandine û heta bi tehl û tirşiya çendîn salan pêwistiyek pêk aniye.
669. he published and up to the bitterness and sourness of several years fulfilled a necessity.

670. Ez fikirîm û di dawiyê de têgihîştim ku çapkirina Şerefnameyê, ji bilî feydeya wê ya mezin û piralî ya ji bo zanyariya dîroka di derheqê Kurdan de, gelek û gelek pêwist e.
670. I thought and in the end comprehended that the printing of the Şerefname, aside from its great and multifaceted benefit for the science of history regarding the Kurds, is very and very necessary.

671. Ji ber vê, min biryar da ku ez nusxeya Farisî bi wergera wê ve belav bikim û çend meseleyên felsefî, dîrokî û coxrafî jî têkim ser wê beşa ku ez ê biweşînim, ji bilî kutayiyê, ku dawî bi Şerefnameyê tîne, ew jî ku ez ê çap bikim, ji bo dîroka Kurdan gelek girîng e, çunkê:
671. Because of this, I decided that I distribute the Persian copy along with its translation and add several philosophical, historical, and geographical issues to that part that I will publish, aside from the short part (kutayi), which brings an end to the Şerefname, that too which I will print, is very important for the history of the Kurds, because:

672. Behsa her eşîretê cihê cihê tê de ye û ez bi behsa serpêhatiya jiyana nivîskar dawî lê tînim û kutayiya wê jî dikeve ser cilda duduyan.
672. The discussion of every tribe is in it separately and I bring an end to it with the discussion of the adventure of the author's life and its short part (kutayi) as well falls onto the second volume.

673. Vêca va ez ê bi çend bêjeyan ronî bikim ku ji bo belavkirina Farisiya wê ya esli, ka gelo min çi kiriye:
673. So here I will illuminate with several words that for the distribution of its original Persian, what indeed I have done:

678. It starts from the middle of the discussion of the Mehmûdî Tribe and in the discussion of the Siyamensorî Tribe it gets cut off (See: First Part, my Persian edition, pages: 304-327).

679. 2. The Manuscript of Mr. Khanikov.

680. Vi zanayê han ê Rojhilatnas, di sala 1854'an de, li Pîranê konsolosê giştî yê Rûsyayê bûye.
680. This very Orientalist scholar, in the year 1854, in Pîran was the general consul of Russia.

682. Honorable Khanikov, who everyone knows what kind of man he is in the path of science, until the day I finished its printing, laid his manuscripts before my hands.

683. Destnivîsa Xanikov, di 19'yê meha Şe'bana sala 1252'yê koçî (1837'ê zayînî) de mirovekî navê wî Mehmûd Riza yê kurê Sabir 'Elî Kerbelayî (Qerbelayî), li ser fermana Rewşen Efendî yê ji gundê Dîlmekan, li ber destnivîsek Şerefnameyê nivîsandiye, ku dîroka wê nusxayê digihîje sala 1007'ê koçî û di dawiya wê de jî wiha nivîsandiye:
683. The manuscript of Khanikov, on the 19th of the month of Sha'ban of the Hijri year 1252 (1837 Gregorian), a man whose name was Mehmûd Riza the son of Sabir 'Elî Kerbelayî (Qerbelayî), upon the order of Rewşen Efendî from the village of Dîlmekan, wrote it from a manuscript of the Şerefname, that the date of that copy reaches the Hijri year 1007 and at the end of it as well he wrote thus:

684. Ev gotina han rast e.
684. This very word is true.

Çunkî di dawiya destnivîsê de wiha hatiye nivîsandin: "Di dawiya Meha Şeşekan (Muherrem) a sala 1007'an a koça Pêxember -silavên Xwedê li ser bin- li bajarê Bedlîsê -Xwedê ji qeza belayan biparêze- bi destê nivîskarê kitêbê bi xwe -her wext payedar be û destê wî sax be- hatiye rastkirin û dawî bi vê kitêbê hat."

Because at the end of the manuscript it is written thus: "At the end of the Month of Şeşekan (Muharram) of the year 1007 of the migration of the Prophet -peace of God be upon him- in the city of Bedlîs -may God protect it from accidents and calamities- by the hand of the author of the book himself -may he always be enduring and may his hand be healthy- it was corrected and an end came to this book."

758. Destpêka gotara min bi sipasî ji wî Xwedayî re dest pê dike ku pesnê Wî bi dilekî ronak ve wek roja bi tîrêj erd û asîman ronî dike, ew xwedan şukir û senayê ku hemû payeberz û serbilind li ber dergahê Wî destpençe û sernizm disekinin.
758. The beginning of my speech starts with thanks to that God whose praise with a bright heart like the beaming sun illuminates the earth and sky, that owner of gratitude and praise that all the high-ranking and proud stand with crossed hands and bowed heads before His court.

759. Ew, karmendek e ku; firaxek nalînê ji bo pena û pêsîrê giyanê Adem ji heriyê çêkiriye, bi tîna tîrêja merhemeta xwe hişk kiriye û bi çav li ser gerandinek wisan rêk û pêk kiriye û ewqas rêz lê girtiye ku, layîqê meclisa baregahê pir şukur û sena ya Wî be û li ber pêşkên textê Yezdan de bi qedir û hêja cî jê re veqetandiye.
759. He, is a worker who; made a vessel of groaning from mud for the refuge and bosom of Adam's soul, hardened it with the heat of the beam of His mercy and with such an overseeing eye made it orderly and proper and respected it so much that, it may be worthy of the assembly of His court of much gratitude and praise and before the front of the throne of God He separated an honorable and worthy place for it.

760. Ew, hunermendek e ku: di axa reş a şilekî de xêr û bereketek wisan rijandiye ku bi hezaran hezar barên mirarî û cewher di bazara durrnasan di muqabilê wê de ne tiştek bin.
760. He, is an artist who: into the wet black earth poured such goodness and blessing that thousands of thousands of loads of pearls and jewels in the market of pearl-connoisseurs in exchange for it would be nothing.

761. Ew, baladestek e ku; ew padişah, xan, beg, serdar û serwerên bi pozbilindî, kibîrî û şanazî xwe bi ser bindestan radigirin û henekan li ha û li wir dikin, li hemberî hêz û desthilata Wî rût, pût û bêser û ber in.
761. He, is a superior who; those kings, xans, begs, commanders and sovereigns who with high-nosedness (arrogance), pride and boasting hold themselves over the subjugated and make jokes here and there, against His power and authority are naked, idols and in a mess.

762. Ew parsek û bêpenayên ku bi çavên çakî li destê bexşînder ê Xwedayê tik û tenê dinêrin û destên niyazan tenê ber bi baregaha Wî ya şukurdar bilind dikin û ji wî pê ve karên wan bi tu kesî hel nabe û her tenê Ew e baregaha wan a dawiyê.
762. Those beggars and refuge-less ones who with eyes of goodness look at the granting hand of the one and only God and raise the hands of supplications only towards His grateful court and aside from Him their affairs cannot be solved by anyone and He alone is their final court.

763. Ger çendîn bi şiklî û bi dêmen, bê paye, bê ser û ber, şerpeze û perîşan in, lê di rastiya xwe de padişahên serbilind û dilxweş her ew in.
763. No matter how much in form and in appearance, they are without rank, in a mess, scattered and miserable, but in their truth the proud and happy kings are always them.

764. With knowledge He elevates some temporarily, places them over societies and for testing, gives their yarn full wind (lets them loose), and turns His back to them for a time.

765. In the result He sends the Angel of Death to them, takes them out of the assembly of life and sends them towards the grave of the world and entrusts them to the narrow and cold grave.

766. On the day of question and account He asks them of their good and evils and before His high court shamelessly and flawlessly He counts their good and evils to them, the healthy and firm He makes guests of Paradise and the rotten and decayed He sends towards Hell.

767. He, is a pattern-maker who; from a single pair of male and female made many various tribes and clans: white, red, yellow, gray, black and brown from the generations and races of mankind, so that they may know themselves and their owners.

768. Ji ber kar û barên jiyanê ku ji hemû serekî ve rêk û pêk be, bend û girêdanên jinnameya mirovayetiyê ji ber hev neveqete û ji hev cihê nebe, ew dam û dezgeha mezin û giran û ew xanedanên pirr xîzan û avahî bi awirekî pirr lerzîn netefe, herkesê li gor pêwistiyên jiyanê hînê tiştekî kiriye û pala wan daye cure zanistek û erkê fermanek hêja bi wan sipartiye.

769. He filled the heart of girls and boys with the heat and hills (flames) of love and filled them with the beam of the longing of reaching one another.

874. The beams of mercy of the one and only God moved, they shook the strong wind of goodness and joy, the darkness of oppression and the dark black clouds of unpleasantness were so utterly scattered that their tracks and traces did not remain either.

876. In the shadow of that holy Shah heart-strength (courage) and discomfort went from the memory of all people, the firing of the casting of hearts and misery lifted at once and the poor, destitute, wretched and troubled, all became full and satisfied and with happy heart and bright eyes led their lives.

879. The nightingale, took off the clothes of misery and melancholy from itself, tore the curtain of silence and very openly and clearly opposite the rose began saying its heart-desires, sang and chirped for it.

882.
