Lesson 1
Pronouns and "To Be" Verb

1. Personal Pronouns in Kurmanji Kurdish

Let's start by learning the basic personal pronouns in Kurmanji Kurdish:

Kurdish English
Ez I
Tu You (singular)
Ew He/She/It
Em We
Hûn You (plural)
Ew They

Note: In Kurmanji Kurdish, "Ew" is used for both the third person singular (he/she/it) and the third person plural (they).

2. The "To Be" Verb in Kurmanji Kurdish

In Kurmanji Kurdish, the "to be" verb changes depending on the pronoun it's used with. Here are the present tense forms:

Pronoun Form English
Ez im I am
Tu î You are
Ew e He/She/It is
Em in We are
Hûn in You (plural) are
Ew in They are

3. Variations After Vowels

When a word ends in a vowel, the "to be" forms change as follows:

Standard After Vowel Example
im (I am) me mamoste + im → mamoste me
î (you are) yî mamoste + î → mamoste yî
e (he/she/it is) ye mamoste + e → mamoste ye
in (we/you/they are) ne mamoste + in → mamoste ne

This change happens because Kurdish avoids having two vowels next to each other across word boundaries.

4. Examples with "xwendekar" (student) and "mamoste" (teacher)

Examples with "xwendekar" (student)

"Xwendekar" ends in a consonant ('r'), so we use the standard forms:

Ez xwendekar im. (I am a student.)

Tu xwendekar î. (You are a student.)

Ew xwendekar e. (He/She is a student.)

Em xwendekar in. (We are students.)

Hûn xwendekar in. (You [plural] are students.)

Ew xwendekar in. (They are students.)

Examples with "mamoste" (teacher)

"Mamoste" ends in a vowel ('e'), so we use the forms for after vowels:

Ez mamoste me. (I am a teacher.)

Tu mamoste yî. (You are a teacher.)

Ew mamoste ye. (He/She is a teacher.)

Em mamoste ne. (We are teachers.)

Hûn mamoste ne. (You [plural] are teachers.)

Ew mamoste ne. (They are teachers.)

Notice how the "to be" verb changes from "im" to "me", "î" to "yî", "e" to "ye", and "in" to "ne" when it comes after a vowel.
