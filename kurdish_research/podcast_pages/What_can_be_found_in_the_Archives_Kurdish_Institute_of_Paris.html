What can be found in the Archives Kurdish Institute of Paris

Description

Established in 1983, the Kurdish Institute of Paris serves as a vital institution dedicated to preserving and promoting Kurdish language, culture, and history. Its extensive archives encompass a diverse range of materials, including textual documents, audiovisual collections, artworks, and personal papers, spanning from the early 20th century to the present day and covering both Kurdistan and the diaspora. Through its Kurdish Digital Library (BNK), the Institute has digitized a significant portion of its holdings, making over a million pages accessible globally to researchers and the public. Beyond archiving, the Institute actively disseminates knowledge and fosters cultural engagement through academic journals, book publications, cultural programming, and public events, establishing itself as a crucial center for Kurdish studies and a de facto cultural embassy for a stateless people, though it faces ongoing financial challenges.
