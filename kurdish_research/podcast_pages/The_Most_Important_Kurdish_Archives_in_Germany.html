The Most Important Kurdish Archives in Germany

Description

These sources discuss the critical importance of Kurdish archives in Germany for preserving cultural identity and contesting historical narratives, highlighting that Germany hosts a large Kurdish diaspora, necessitating robust preservation efforts. The text explores the "archival turn" in Kurdish Studies, which emphasizes using diverse sources beyond traditional state archives, including materials from diaspora organizations and personal collections. Key institutional archives like EZKS and NAVEND are identified as significant centers for Kurdish studies, holding extensive libraries and fostering research, while the text also acknowledges the value of community-based collections and the challenges presented by confiscated archives, such as those from Mezopotamien Verlag. Finally, the sources note the growing role of digital archives and the contributions of institutions like the German National Library in documenting Kurdish experiences and output in Germany, all within a complex environment marked by political sensitivities and preservation challenges.
