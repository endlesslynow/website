Afrin Alevis Before 2010

Download Audio File

Description

These sources describe the Alevi community residing in the Afrin region of Syria prior to 2010. They detail the history and Anatolian origins of this predominantly Kurdish-speaking group, highlighting their distinct religious beliefs and practices which differ from both Sunni Muslims and Syria's Alawite population. The materials examine the social structure and demographic presence of Afrin's Alevis, noting their concentration in the Maabatli sub-district and connections to Aleppo. Furthermore, the sources explore the political and economic context in which the community lived under Ba'athist rule, facing discrimination as Kurds while also maintaining their unique religious identity.
