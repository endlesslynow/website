The Regional Ties of Afrin before the French Mandate Period

Download Audio File

Description

This historical report explores the pre-1920 connections of the Afrin/Kurd Dagh region within the Ottoman Empire. It defines the geographical area in present-day Syria and Turkey and details its administrative integration primarily under the Eyalet and later Vilayet of Aleppo. The study examines the region's demographic makeup, noting the long-standing Kurdish presence alongside Yazidi and other communities, and analyzes its agrarian-based economy, highlighting its crucial role as an olive oil producer for Aleppo's trade networks. Finally, it investigates the social and political dynamics, including tribal structures and the evolving relationship between local Kurdish society and Ottoman rule, before the establishment of modern borders.
