How did the Baath regime in Syria Bring Arabs to Afrin and Displace Others

Download Audio File

Description

The provided source details the Baathist regime's systematic Arabization policies in Syria before 2010, focusing on its approach to the Kurdish population. It explains how the regime, driven by pan-Arab nationalism, viewed the Kurdish minority as a threat and implemented strategies to suppress their identity and displace them. The document compares two distinct methods: the direct demographic engineering of the "Arab Belt" project in Jazira province, involving land confiscation and Arab settlement, and a more indirect strategy in Afrin district. In Afrin, the regime employed cultural suppression, economic marginalization to induce migration, and legal restrictions on property rights to achieve gradual displacement without overt settlement, highlighting the adaptable nature of its discriminatory policies.
