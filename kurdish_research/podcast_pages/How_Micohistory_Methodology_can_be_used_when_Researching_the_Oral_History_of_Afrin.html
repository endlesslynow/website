How Micohistory Methodology can be used when Researching the Oral History of Afrin

Download Audio File

Description

The source proposes a research methodology combining microhistory and oral history to understand the recent transformations in Afrin, Syria, particularly after 2018. It argues that focusing on individual experiences and personal narratives through oral testimonies, while connecting them to broader societal patterns, can effectively document the impact of displacement, human rights abuses, and cultural suppression. The text highlights the ethical considerations crucial when researching a traumatized population and suggests specific areas of focus, like the experiences of displaced families or changes in cultural practices, to provide a more nuanced and human-centered historical record that challenges dominant accounts and preserves memory.
