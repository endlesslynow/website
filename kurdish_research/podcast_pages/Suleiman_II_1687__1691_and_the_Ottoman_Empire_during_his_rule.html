Suleiman II 1687 1691 and the Ottoman Empire during his rule

Description

The source provides an extensive historical analysis of the four-year reign of Sultan Suleiman II of the Ottoman Empire, which lasted from 1687 to 1691, a time marked by profound crisis. The text explains that Suleiman II was profoundly ill-prepared for the throne, having spent nearly forty years isolated in the Kafes (the imperial cage), yet he was forced into power following a devastating military defeat and subsequent army mutiny. Recognizing his own lack of experience, the sultan made the crucial decision to delegate unprecedented authority to the highly capable Grand Vizier Köprülü Fazıl Mustafa Pasha. This partnership enabled a dramatic but brief Ottoman recovery, which included comprehensive fiscal reforms, such as overhauling the *cizye* tax, and a critical military turnaround highlighted by the recapture of Belgrade in 1690. Ultimately, this resurgence proved ephemeral, as the empire quickly collapsed back into crisis following the death of Fazıl Mustafa Pasha in battle in 1691.
