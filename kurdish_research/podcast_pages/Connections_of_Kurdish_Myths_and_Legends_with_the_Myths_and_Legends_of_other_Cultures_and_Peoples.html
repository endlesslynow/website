Connections of Kurdish Myths and Legends with the Myths and Legends of other Cultures and Peoples

Download Audio File

Description

This analysis examines Kurdish mythology, describing it as a rich blend of ancient beliefs, legends, and practices influenced by the Kurds' location at a cultural crossroads. It explores origin narratives that connect Kurds to figures from Abrahamic traditions, indigenous tribal stories, and the heroic Kawa myth. The text highlights central figures like the resistance hero Kawa, the wise snake-woman Shahmaran, and the epic hero Mîrza Mihemed, alongside legendary creatures and the tyrannical Zahhak. It details significant narratives and traditions, particularly the Newroz festival, the epic love story Mem û Zîn, and the distinct indigenous faiths of Yazidism and Yarsanism, noting their unique cosmologies and potential ancient roots. The analysis emphasizes the interwoven threads connecting Kurdish mythology to Iranian, Mesopotamian, Anatolian, and Caucasian traditions, demonstrating how Kurdish culture has both borrowed from and uniquely adapted regional themes and figures. Ultimately, the sources present Kurdish mythology as a dynamic reflection of the people's history, identity, and resilience.
