1. Adjectival Pronoun ضمیر وصفی

The Adjectival Pronominal affix is the word کی -ki, meaning 'the — which' or 'that which'. It can be attached to nouns and pronouns in two ways: by putting them either in the Genitive or in the Locative case.

When used with the Genitive case, it acts like a noun and means 'that which belongs to'. When used with the Locative case, it can be either a noun meaning 'that which exists' or an adjective meaning 'the — which exists'.

Examples:

Ottoman TurkishTransliterationTranslation
باباbabafather
باباكbabanınof the father
باباكیbabanın-kithat which belongs to the father
بابدهbabadain the father
بابدکیbabadakithat which exists in the father's possession
بندکیbendekithat which I have

7. Reflexive Pronouns ضمیر تاکیدی

The English words myself, himself, yourself etc. are called Reflexive Pronouns when they refer to the same person as the subject. They are expressed in Turkish by the pronoun کندی kendi:

EnglishOttoman Turkish
I myself / Myselfبن کندم / کندم
You yourself / Yourselfسن کندن / کندن
He himself / Himselfاو کندیسی / کندیسی
We ourselves / Ourselvesبز کندمز / کندمز
You yourselves / Yourselvesسز کندنز / کندنز
They themselves / Themselvesاونلر کندلری / کندلری
