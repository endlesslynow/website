Grammar Points

The Definite Article

There is no Definite Article in Ottoman Turkish; all nouns, when used alone in a sentence, are usually considered as definite.

Ottoman Turkish Latin Script English
بابا baba the father
آنا ana the mother
قرداش qardash the brother

The Indefinite Article

The Indefinite Article is بر bir (a, an).

Ottoman Turkish Latin Script English
بر آت bir at a horse
بر كوپك bir köpek a dog
بر قيز bir qiz a girl
بر آدم bir adem a man

Adjectives

The Adjective always precedes the noun.

Ottoman Turkish Latin Script English
كوزل güzel beautiful
اي، ايي iyi good
كوتو kötü bad

Adjectives with Nouns

Ottoman Turkish Latin Script English
كوزل قيز güzel qiz the beautiful girl
ايي آدم i-yi adem the good man
كوتو چوجق kötü chojuq a bad boy

Gender

As in English, there is no unnatural distinction of Gender in Ottoman Turkish, that is to say: the names of males are masculine; those of females feminine, and those of inanimate objects, neuter.

Ottoman Turkish Gender Latin Script Translation
بابا masculine baba father
آنا feminine ana mother
قيز feminine qiz girl
اوغلان masculine oghlan boy
دكز neuter deniz sea
شهر neuter shehir city
كوي neuter köy village

Personal Pronouns

Ottoman Turkish Latin Script English
بن ben I
سن sen you (singular/informal)
او o he, she, it
بز biz we
سز siz you (plural/formal)
اونلر onlar they

Demonstrative Pronouns

Ottoman Turkish Latin Script English
بو bou this
شو shou that (near by)
او o that (distant)

Present Tense of the Turkish Substantive Verb

Affirmative Present

Ottoman Turkish Latin Script English
بن ایم ben' im I am
سن سین sen' sin you are (singular/informal)
او در o' dour he/she/it is
بز ایز biz' iz we are
سز سکز siz' siniz you are (plural/formal)
انلر درلر onlar' dirlar they are

Question Formation

The question is expressed by adding می mi, mou after the word emphasized by the question.

Interrogative Affirmative Present

Ottoman Turkish Latin Script English
بن می یم؟ ben' mi yim? Am I?
سن می سین؟ sen' mi sin? Are you? (singular/informal)
او می در؟ o' mou dour? Is he/she/it?
بز می یز؟ biz' mi yiz? Are we?
سز می سکز؟ siz' mi siniz? Are you? (plural/formal)
انلر می درلر؟ onlar' mi dirlar? Are they?

Copula Pronunciation Note

The Copula, or the "to be" verb, like that of mi می, is governed by the preceding vowel, and is: dir, dir, dour, dür, as the case may be.

Ottoman Turkish Latin Script English
بن می یم؟ ben' mi yim? Am I?
کل بیاض می در؟ gül beyaz' mi dir? Is the rose white?
بو بر کل می در؟ bou bir gül' mü dir? Is this a rose?
کل بو می دور؟ gül bou' mou dour? Is this the rose?
