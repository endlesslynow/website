The Climate History of the Ancient Middle East and how it Affected Politics

Description

The provided text examines the profound influence of climate change on the rise and fall of civilizations in the Ancient Near East, covering the Bronze and Iron Ages. It explains how paleoclimatology, using proxies like speleothems and sediment cores, reconstructs past environmental conditions, revealing significant aridification events. The source highlights two major climate-induced collapses: the 4.2 ka BP event which impacted the Akkadian Empire and Old Kingdom Egypt, and the Late Bronze Age megadrought that led to a widespread systemic breakdown. Ultimately, the text argues that while climate posed immense challenges, human societies demonstrated remarkable resilience and adapted through technological innovations and new sociopolitical structures, such as the Neo-Assyrian Empire.
