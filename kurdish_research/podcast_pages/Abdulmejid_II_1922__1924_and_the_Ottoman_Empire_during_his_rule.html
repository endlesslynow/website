Abdulmejid II 1922 1924 and the Ottoman Empire during his rule

Description

The source provides a comprehensive analysis of Abdulmejid II, focusing on his brief and pivotal time as the final Caliph of the Ottoman Dynasty between 1922 and 1924, a period marking the transition from empire to the Turkish Republic. It explores the paradox of his position as a figurehead without temporal power, highlighting his intellectual and artistic background, which included mastery of painting and European culture. The text details the abolition of the Sultanate by the Grand National Assembly, which led to the unprecedented experiment of the "Republican Caliphate" before the spiritual office was itself abolished due to political tensions and perceived foreign interference, notably the "Aga Khan Letter". Finally, the document recounts Abdulmejid II's exile from Turkey and the decade-long struggle after his death for his body to be buried in Istanbul, ultimately resulting in his interment in Medina.
