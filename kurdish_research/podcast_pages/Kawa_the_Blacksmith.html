Description

These sources comprehensively examine the legend of Kawa the Blacksmith, a pivotal heroic figure in Persian and Kurdish mythology who leads a revolt against the tyrannical King Zahhak. The texts highlight Kawa's transformation from a humble craftsman to a symbol of justice, resistance, and freedom, contrasting his popular origin with aristocratic epic heroes. They detail how his story is prominently featured in Ferdowsi's Shahnameh while also acknowledging variations in Kurdish tradition, where Kawa's role in defeating the tyrant and the connection to Kurdish ancestry are often more central. The sources also explore the deep symbolic links between Kawa's victory and the Newroz festival, particularly the symbolism of fire, and touch upon scholarly theories regarding the myth's origins and evolution. Ultimately, Kawa is presented as a timeless icon whose legacy endures in art, literature, and contemporary struggles for self-determination.
