Description

The provided text extensively explores the Kurdish Dengbêj tradition, a vital form of oral culture featuring singer-poets who preserve history, legends, and social commentary. The study defines Dengbêjî, examines its historical roots and key figures like Evdalê Zeynikê and Ayşe Şan, and analyzes its thematic, narrative, and musical characteristics. It highlights the crucial role of Dengbêj in safeguarding Kurdish language and memory during periods of suppression and discusses the politicization and heritagization of the tradition in modern times. Finally, the text considers the contemporary status, revival efforts, and future challenges facing this enduring cultural practice.
