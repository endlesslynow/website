The Geography, Lifeways, and Culture of Afrin

Description

This report explores the pre-2010 relationship between the geography and traditional lifeways of the Afrin region in northwestern Syria, also known as Kurd Dagh. It details how the mountainous terrain, fertile valleys, the Afrin River, and Mediterranean climate shaped everything from agriculture, particularly olive cultivation, to settlement patterns and vernacular architecture. The text highlights a deep human-environment connection, influencing social structures, daily diet, and cultural traditions like the Nowruz festival. Essentially, the sources describe how the unique landscape of Afrin profoundly impacted the resilient and distinct cultural identity and practices of its inhabitants.
