Mehmed V 1909 1918 and the Ottoman Empire during his rule

Description

The provided text is an exhaustive research report analyzing the nine-year reign of Sultan Mehmed V Reşad (1909–1918), framing him as the "Twilight Sovereign" who presided over the final, catastrophic decade of the Ottoman Empire. The document details the Sultan’s paradoxical personal life—his thirty years of forced seclusion in the *Kafes* and his spiritual identity as a devout Mevlevi Sufi—in contrast with the brutal political realities of his era. It charts the rapid decline of the empire, starting with the Sultan’s ascension as a constitutional monarch stripped of power by the Committee of Union and Progress (CUP), through the trauma of the Balkan Wars, and culminating in the decision to enter World War I. Crucially, the report covers the period of the CUP dictatorship, highlighting the Sultan's powerlessness during the issuance of the Jihad Proclamation and, most tragically, the execution of the Armenian Genocide and subsequent demographic re-engineering of Anatolia before the Sultan’s death in 1918.
