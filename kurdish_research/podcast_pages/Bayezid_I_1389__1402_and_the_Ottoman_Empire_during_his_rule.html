Bayezid I 1389 1402 and the Ottoman Empire during his rule

Description

The provided text offers an extensive overview of Sultan Bayezid I's reign (1389–1402), highlighting his pivotal role in transforming the Ottoman state from a frontier principality into a centralized empire. It details his aggressive dual-front expansion in both the Balkans and Anatolia, characterized by swift military campaigns that earned him the moniker "the Thunderbolt." The text also explores his administrative and military reforms, such as the professionalization of the Janissaries and the institutionalization of the devşirme system, which aimed to consolidate sultanic power. Despite his significant achievements, his hubris and strategic miscalculations led to a catastrophic defeat and capture by Timur at the Battle of Ankara, plunging the empire into a period of civil war known as the Interregnum. Ultimately, the sources convey a nuanced legacy of a ruler who, despite his personal downfall, laid crucial institutional foundations that enabled the eventual resurgence and continued growth of the Ottoman Empire.
