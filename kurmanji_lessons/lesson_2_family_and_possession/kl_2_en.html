Cousin Terms

In Kurdish, there are specific terms for different types of cousins:

Kurdish English
pismam paternal male cousin (son of paternal uncle)
dotmam paternal female cousin (daughter of paternal uncle)
pisxal maternal male cousin (son of maternal uncle)
dotxal maternal female cousin (daughter of maternal uncle)
pismêt son of paternal aunt
dotmêt daughter of paternal aunt
pisxaltî son of maternal aunt
dotxaltî daughter of maternal aunt

Note: In Kurdish cousin terms, "pis" means "son of" and "dot" means "daughter of". The second part indicates the relationship to the parent (mam, xal, mêt, xaltî).

Rahênan: Form the Correct Cousin Terms

Complete the blanks to form the correct Kurdish terms for cousins by combining "pis" (son of) or "dot" (daughter of) with the appropriate family term:

1. + mam = (son of paternal uncle)
2. + mam = (daughter of paternal uncle)
3. + xal = (son of maternal uncle)
4. + xal = (daughter of maternal uncle)

2. Indefinite Articles (Vegetandekên nebinavkirî)

In Kurmanji Kurdish, indefinite articles are added as suffixes to nouns:

Vegetandekên nebinavkirî (Indefinite Articles) Mînak (Example) English Translation
-eke (mê/yekjimar) (feminine/singular) Xwişkeke min heye I have a sister
-ekî (nêr/yekjimar) (masculine/singular) Birayekî min heye I have a brother
-ine (pirjimar) (plural) Hevaline min hene I have friends

Rahênan: Add the Correct Indefinite Article

Complete each sentence by adding the appropriate indefinite article suffix (-eke for feminine singular, -ekî for masculine singular, or -ine for plural):

1. Keç min ji Amedê ye. (A daughter of mine is from Amed.) [feminine]
2. Bira min nexweş e. (A brother of mine is sick.) [masculine]
3. Heval min li vir in. (Some friends of mine are here.) [plural]
4. Mamoste min kurd e. (A teacher of mine is Kurdish.) [masculine]
5. Pirtûk min li ser kurdî ye. (A book of mine is about Kurdish.) [feminine]

3. The Existence Verb "heye/hene"

In Kurmanji Kurdish, we use "heye" (singular) and "hene" (plural) to express existence or possession:

Form Usage English
heye singular there is, has
hene plural there are, have

Examples:

Using "heye" and "hene" in sentences:

Xwişka min heye. (I have a sister.)

Du birayên min hene. (I have two brothers.)

Mala me heye. (We have a house.)

Sê zarokên wî hene. (He has three children.)

Important: "Heye" is used when talking about one thing, and "hene" is used when talking about multiple things. The form depends on how many things there are, not on who possesses them.

4. Possessive Cases (Qertafên tewangê)

The possessive forms change depending on gender and number:

Qertafên tewangê (Possessive Cases) Mînak (Example) English Translation
-ê (mê-yekjimar) Bavê Dilanê parêzer e.
Bavê wê parêzer e.		 Dilane's father is a lawyer.
Her father is a lawyer.
-î (nêr-yekjimar) Diya Serhatî bijîşk e.
Diya wî bijîşk e.		 Serhat's mother is a doctor.
His mother is a doctor.
-an (pirjimar) Keça mêvanan nexweş e.
Keça wan nexweş e.		 The guests' daughter is sick.
Their daughter is sick.

Note: These possessive forms change depending on gender (masculine or feminine) and number (singular or plural).

Rahênan: Add the Correct Possessive Suffix

Complete each sentence by adding the appropriate possessive suffix (-ê, -î, or -an) to show the relationship between the nouns:

1. Keça Dilan nexweş e. (Dilan's daughter is sick.)
2. Bavê Serhat mamoste ye. (Serhat's father is a teacher.)
3. Mala mêvan mezin e. (The guests' house is big.)
4. Diya zarok li vir e. (The children's mother is here.)
5. Xwişka Azad bijîşk e. (Azad's sister is a doctor.)

5. Connective Izafe System (Vegetandek)

The izafe system connects words to show relationships:

Vegetandek (Connective Izafe) Mînak (Example) English Translation
-a (yekjimar-mê) Meta min My aunt
-ê (yekjimar-nêr) Bavê min My father
-ên (pirjimar) Xwişkên min My sisters

Examples in sentences:

Using the izafe system in complete sentences:

Meta min mamoste ye. (My aunt is a teacher.)

Bavê min karker e. (My father is a worker.)

Xwişkên min xwendekar in. (My sisters are students.)

Rahênan: Add the Correct Izafe Connector

3. Xwişk min xwendekar in. (My sisters are students.)
4. Mal wan mezin e. (Their house is big.)
5. Zarok te çawa ne? (How are your children?)
