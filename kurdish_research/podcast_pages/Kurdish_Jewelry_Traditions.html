Kurdish Jewelry Traditions

Description

These sources provide an overview of the rich and diverse traditions of Kurdish jewelry, highlighting its significance in Kurdish culture. They explore the historical roots of Kurdish adornment, tracing influences from ancient civilizations and successive empires, while also discussing the various materials and techniques used in its creation, such as gold, silver, turquoise, filigree, and niello. The text also examines the symbolic meanings embedded in Kurdish jewelry, including its role in denoting status, offering protection against evil, and incorporating cultural motifs, and details its crucial social and ceremonial functions, particularly in weddings and festivals like Newroz. Finally, the sources touch upon regional variations across Kurdistan and the contemporary evolution of these traditions, showcasing how modern artists and cultural initiatives are working to preserve and adapt this heritage.
