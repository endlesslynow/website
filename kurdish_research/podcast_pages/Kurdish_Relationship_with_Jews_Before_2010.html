Kurdish Relationship with Jews Before 2010

Description

The provided text examines the complex relationship between Kurdish and Jewish peoples, tracing its evolution through two significant phases. Initially, it describes a millennia-long period of organic coexistence in Mesopotamia, highlighting the deep cultural exchange while maintaining distinct ethno-religious identities. This ancient bond, rooted in shared geography and a sense of being non-Arab minorities, laid the groundwork for future interactions. The text then transitions to the 20th century, detailing the mass exodus of Kurdistani Jews from their homeland due to political upheaval and persecution, particularly in Iraq, and their migration to Israel. Finally, it outlines the emergence of a strategic, clandestine alliance between the State of Israel and the Iraqi Kurdish national movement after 1948, driven by mutual geopolitical necessity and Israel's "periphery doctrine," which later transformed into a more open partnership by 2010.
