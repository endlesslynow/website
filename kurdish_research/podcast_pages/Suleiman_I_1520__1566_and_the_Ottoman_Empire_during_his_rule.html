Suleiman I 1520 1566 and the Ottoman Empire during his rule

Description

The source provides an extensive overview of the 46-year reign of Sultan Suleiman I (1520–1566), detailing why this period represents the "Golden Age" and zenith of the Ottoman Empire. It examines his dual identity as "the Magnificent" (military conqueror and cultural patron) and "Kanuni" (the Lawgiver), stressing his comprehensive legal reforms that unified the empire's administrative system. Furthermore, the text explores the political drama within his court, highlighting the immense influence of his wife, Hürrem Sultan, and the brutal succession crises that led to the execution of his sons. Finally, the document discusses Suleiman’s military dominance on three continents and the cultural brilliance of the era, epitomized by the architecture of Mimar Sinan and the flourishing of imperial arts.
