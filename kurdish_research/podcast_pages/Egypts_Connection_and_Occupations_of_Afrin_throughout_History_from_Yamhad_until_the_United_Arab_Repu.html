Egypts Connection and Occupations of Afrin throughout History from Yamhad until the United Arab Republic

Description

This report examines Egypt's indirect influence on Syria's Afrin region before 2010, emphasizing that Egypt's actions were driven by its national interests and relationship with Syria, not specific Kurdish policy. Under Gamal Abdel Nasser, the United Arab Republic led to the suppression of Kurdish identity and political organizations in Syria, inadvertently paving the way for further discrimination. During Anwar Sadat's presidency, Egypt's focus shifted inward, resulting in disengagement from Syrian affairs and no impact on the Kurdish situation. Finally, Hosni Mubarak's mediation in the 1998 Turkey-Syria crisis, though not directly aimed at Kurdish issues, unintentionally facilitated the creation of the PYD, a key Kurdish political force in Afrin.
