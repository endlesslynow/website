Minorities of Afrin

Download Audio File

Description

Prior to 2010, the Afrin District in northwestern Syria was characterized by a large Kurdish majority alongside various smaller ethnic and religious minority groups. Official Syrian data from this period is limited in providing detailed ethnic or religious breakdowns, necessitating reliance on non-state estimates. Notable minorities included Arabs, with varied histories of settlement including state-sponsored relocation, and Turkmen, who had ancient roots in specific villages like Maryamin. Significant religious and ethno-religious minorities were also present, including a substantial Yazidi population with deep historical ties and a concentrated Kurdish Alevi community who found refuge in the area, as well as various Christian denominations. Despite Syrian state policies that often discriminated against the Kurdish population and, by extension, other groups, local accounts frequently depicted peaceful coexistence and tolerance among Afrin's diverse communities before the major conflicts began.
