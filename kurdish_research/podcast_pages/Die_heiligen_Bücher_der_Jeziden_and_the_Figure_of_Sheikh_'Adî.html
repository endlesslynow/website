Die heiligen Bücher der Jeziden and the Figure of Sheikh 'Adî

Download Audio File

Description

The source provides a critical analysis of early 20th-century Orientalist scholarship on the Yazidi faith, focusing specifically on Maximilian Bittner's influential 1913 publication, Die heiligen Bücher der Jeziden. The text argues that Bittner's work, which was supported by a prestigious academic institution, canonized inauthentic Yazidi texts and cemented the erroneous label of "Devil-Worshippers" as an academic term. Furthermore, the analysis explains how this early scholarship was biased toward textual evidence and thus dismissed the fundamentally oral nature of Yazidism. Finally, the source details the syncretic history of the faith's central figure, Sheikh 'Adî, contrasting the historical 12th-century Sufi with the later, divinely venerated Yazidi Saint.
