Mehmed I 1413 1421 and the Ottoman Empire during his rule

Description

This comprehensive text details the reign of Sultan Mehmed I, often called the "Second Founder" of the Ottoman Empire, focusing on his critical role in restoring the state after the devastating Battle of Ankara in 1402 and the subsequent Ottoman Interregnum. The document highlights Mehmed I's strategic acumen during the civil war against his brothers, his pragmatic approach to governance and foreign policy, and his dedication to rebuilding shattered institutions like the timar system. It also covers his efforts to suppress internal rebellions, notably the ideological challenge of Sheikh Bedreddin, and his measured foreign policy, which prioritized stability and consolidation over aggressive expansion, a lesson reinforced by the naval defeat at Gallipoli. Ultimately, the text argues that Mehmed I's reign provided the essential foundation for the Ottoman Empire's future successes and enduring legacy.
