2. Prepositions

There are three basic prepositions, found in all varieties of Kurmanji:

li 'at, in'
ji 'from'
bi 'through, by'

There are many other prepositions in addition to these three basic prepositions. Sometimes they can be combined with the basic prepositions. For example, ser 'on', bin 'under', which may be combined with the basic three prepositions (li ser 'on, about', as in li ser zimanê kurdî 'about/on the Kurdish language') or used alone, as in ser çavan 'on eyes', meaning 'at your disposal', an expression of friendly respect and agreement.

3. Circumpositions

A unique feature of Kurmanji is the common use of circumpositions. Circumpositions are prepositions but instead of being formed before the word, they're formed around the word.

The three ending circumposition pieces (pieces used after the noun) are: ve, de, re

For example:

bi te re 'with you'

ji te re 'for you, to you'

ji îro ve 'from today onwards'

In addition, there are circumpositions with a first element di. Unlike the basic prepositions discussed above, this element can not occur by itself, but only in combination with a final particle:

di mala wê de 'in her house'

di ... re 'through'

The final particles have a vague meaning, approximately as follows: de usually adds a meaning of stationary position; while re adds some sense of togetherness; and finally ve adds some sense of getting away from.

4. Common Prepositions/Circumpositions

Here are some common prepositions and circumpositions with examples of their usage:

Preposition Meaning Example Translation
ba/nik/cem to, towards Ew her roj diçe cem hevalên xwe. Every day he goes to his friends.
bi with, by means of Ez bi bisîkletê diçim dibistanê. I go to school by bicycle.
bi ...re with Bisekîne! Ez jî bi te re têm bazarê. Stop! I too will come to the market with you.
bo for, to Ji kerema xwe bo min çayekî dagire. If you would be so kind, pour a tea for me.
di nav ...de inside of, among Tu çi dikî di nav avê de? What are you putting into the water?
di navbera ...de between Welatê me di navbera Ferat û Dîcleyê de ye. Our homeland is between the Euphrates and the Tigris (rivers).
heta until Ez heta saet nehê şevê kar dikim. I am working until nine O'clock in the evening.
ji from, of Derya ji Qoserê ye. Derya is from Qoser.
ji from, of Mast ji şîr çêdibe. Yoghurt is made of milk.
ji ...re to Ez ji te re dibêjim lê tu ji kesî re nebêje. I tell you but don't you tell it to anyone (lit. no-one).
ji...ve from, since Ji saet heftê sibehê ve kar dikim. I have been working since 7 in morning.
ji bilî except for, other than Di malbata wan de ji bilî wî tu kes kar nake. In their family no-one works except for him.
ji bo for Hemî hewla min ji bo wî ye. All my effort is for him.
li ber in front of Zû were! Li ber bankê li hêviya te me. Come quickly! I am waiting for you in front of the bank.
li dijî against Em li dijî neheqiyê têdikoşin. We struggle against injustice.
ligel with, along with Kunçî jî ligel zarokan dilîze. Kunçî too plays with the children.
li gorî according to Li gorî wî em neheq in. According to him, we are wrong.
li pêş in front of Demhat ji dibistanê de li pêş Rûkenê rûdine. Demhat sits in front of Rûken in the classroom.
li rex beside Malên wan li rex hev in. Their houses are next to each other.
li ser on, over Bîne, deyne li ser vê maseyê. Bring (it) and put (it) on this table!
wekî/mîna like, as Rast e, ez jî wekî te difikirîm. That's right, I too think like you / think the same way as you.
piştî after Piştî saet heftê êvarî vedigere malê. After seven O'clock in the evening (he / she) returns home.

5. Dialogue

Read this dialogue that uses prepositions and verbs from this lesson:

Azad: Ji Stenbolê heta Amedê çiqas dem dikişîne?
How long does it take from Istanbul to Amed?
Rojin: Bi balafirê saetek e, lê bi otobusê bi şev re neh saetan dikişîne.
By plane it's one hour, but by bus through the night it takes nine hours.
Azad: Tu li Stenbolê dijî?
Do you live in Istanbul?
Rojin: Erê, lê ez ji Amedê me. Ji sala 2015an ve li Stenbolê dijîm.
Yes, but I am from Amed. I have been living in Istanbul since 2015.
Azad: Tu bi çi re dixebitî?
What do you work with?
Rojin: Ez di dibistanekê de dixebitim. Ez li dijî neheqiyê têdikoşim.
I work in a school. I struggle against injustice.
Azad: Tu li gorî çi difikirî ji bo vê pirsê?
According to what do you think about this question?
Rojin: Li gorî min, divê em bi hev re kar bikin.
According to me, we should work together.
Azad: Rast e! Di nav civakê de, hemû kes wekî te difikire.
That's right! In society, everyone thinks like you.
Rojin: Piştî dersê, em dikarin hevdu bibînin li ser vê mijarê.
After class, we can see each other about this topic.
