Geography of Afrin and Connection with its History

Description

This academic text examines the profound influence of geography on the Afrin region in northwestern Syria, analyzing how features like the Kurd Dagh mountains, the Afrin River valley, and its strategic location shaped its history before 2010. It highlights how the rugged terrain provided refuge and fostered a distinct Kurdish identity, while the fertile valley supported a resilient agricultural economy, particularly focused on olives. The document explains how this geographical positioning made Afrin a consistent crossroads and contested territory for numerous empires and modern states. Overall, it argues that Afrin's landscape was a defining force, influencing settlement, economy, culture, conflict, and political dynamics across millennia.
