The Life of Prophet Hori the Man of Nebi Hori Not the Historic Site

Description

The provided source explores the syncretic figure of Prophet Hori, also known as the Man of Nebi Hori, located at the ancient site of Cyrrhus in northern Syria. It explains that Hori is not a single historical individual but a composite legend built from three main layers. The first layer identifies Prophet Hori with the Biblical warrior Uriah the Hittite, emphasizing his unwavering loyalty and principles as a moral foundation. The second layer details the Islamic reinterpretation of Uriah's story, aligning it with the doctrine of prophetic infallibility by framing David's actions as a divine test rather than a sin. Finally, the third layer traces the local sanctification of Prophet Hori as a Sufi saint at a Roman-era tomb at Cyrrhus, cementing his place in the regional spiritual landscape during the Mamluk era.
