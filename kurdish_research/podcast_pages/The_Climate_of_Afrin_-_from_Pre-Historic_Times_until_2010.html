The Climate of Afrin - from Pre-Historic Times until 2010

Description

This comprehensive report constructs a multi-millennial climate history of Afrin, Syria, analyzing how climatic fluctuations have profoundly shaped the region's environment and human societies. It begins by characterizing Afrin's hot-summer Mediterranean climate (Csa) and detailing the recent warming and drying trends up to 2010, highlighting the devastating 2006-2010 drought and its role as a "threat multiplier" for societal unrest. The report then explores historical climate shifts, such as the Little Ice Age and its impact on the Ottoman Empire, and the Roman Warm Period which fostered ancient prosperity, contrasting these with periods of severe aridity that caused societal collapses. Finally, it examines the paleoclimate of the Holocene epoch, tracing the origins of agriculture in the region and emphasizing the centrality of olive cultivation and its recurring vulnerability to climate extremes throughout history.
