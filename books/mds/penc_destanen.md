48. ## Destana Şah Bilbil û Mamedê Kurê Mîrê Botan
48. ## The Destan of Şah Bilbil and Mamed the Son of the Mîr of Botan

49. Dibêjin rojekê ji rojên biharê, xortên kurdan derketibûn ser bênderan kêf û şahî dikirin, hinan bi gogê, hinan bi kevirê qewetê û hinan bi siwarbûna ser pişta hespan û cirîdê dilîstin û hunerên xwe şanî hev didan.
49. They say one day of the days of spring, the Kurdish youths had gone out to the threshing floors, having fun and celebrating, some playing with a ball, some with a stone of strength and some by riding on the backs of horses and playing jereed, showing their skills to each other.

50. Mîr Mamed kurê Mîrê Botan jî li hespê xwe siwar dibe û dikeve nav cirîda hespan.
50. Mîr Mamed, son of the Mîr of Botan, also mounts his horse and enters the jereed of horses.

51. Her yek hespê xwe qoş dike bê ka hespê kî beza ye û kî li pêşîya siwaran dixe û digihêje seg û zû vegere cihê xwe.
51. Everyone races their horse to see whose horse is fast and who gets in front of the riders and reaches the mark and returns quickly to their place.

52. Hespan dan ber hev ku çar gavên Lezgîn kurê pîrê bû, ew jî li canîya xwe siwar bû û da nav hespan û kete hofka cirîdê de û bû horbehorba siwaran û bi paşpanîya di zengiwan de li zikê hespê xwe didin qene li pêşîya hespê hevalê xwe bixîne.
52. They raced the horses, when the gallops of Lezgîn, son of the old woman, happened, he too mounted his mare and entered among the horses and entered the arena of the jereed, and there was the clamor of the riders, and with their heels in the stirrups they struck their horses' bellies so that they would get in front of their friend's horse.

53. Di meydanê de, Lezgînê siwarê canîya şînîboz li pêşîya hespê kurê mîr xist, kurê mîr hêrs bû û da ser pişt êû qamçî lê hilda, qene lêxîne.
53. In the square, Lezgîn, the rider of the blue-grey mare, got in front of the mîr's son's horse; the mîr's son became angry and went after him and raised his whip at him, to strike him.

54. Kurê pîrê ji ber kurê mîr revîya û berê xwe da malê.
54. The old woman's son fled from the mîr's son and turned towards home.

55. Zilam û jin derketin ser bênderan nav di xortan didan û ji wan re dilîlandin, pîrê jî ji kêfa kurê xwe re lîland û nav têda.
55. Men and women came out to the threshing floors, encouraging the youths and trilling for them; the old woman also trilled out of joy for her son and encouraged him.

56. Lezgînê pepûk ji tirsa kurê mîr re berê xwe da nav zilam û pîrekan, qene şiva kurê mîr lê nekeve.
56. The wretched Lezgîn, out of fear of the mîr's son, turned toward the men and women, so that the mîr's son's switch would not hit him.

57. Pîrê bi lez û bazdan çû pêşîya kurê mîr û bi rişma hespê wî girt û jê re got: "Te xêr e kuro te wilo daye ser pişta Lezgîn! ma
57. The old woman hurriedly and running went in front of the mîr's son and grabbed his horse's bridle and said to him: "What is it with you, boy, that you are going after Lezgîn like this! Why,

58. çi ji te kiriye, ma surma te şikandîye? pir pir canîya wî li pêşîya hespê te xistîye, de vegere vegere; bê çi paye ye, tu dibêjî qey qîza Mîrê Şêrwan anîye û di hafê de xemilandî ye, de vegere kuro û dev ji kurê min biqere".
58. what has he done to you, has he broken your pride? At most his mare got in front of your horse, come on, go back, go back; what arrogance is this, you act as if you have brought the daughter of the Mîr of Şêrwan and adorned her in the courtyard, come on, go back, boy, and leave my son alone".

59. Mîr Mamed weke tu şivekê li pozê wî bixînî di cihê xwe de sekinî û bi şûn de vegerîya, him ji fedîya û him ji qehra berê xwe da malê, hespê xwe bera êxur da û kete odeya xwe û derî li xwe girt û ponijî.
59. Mîr Mamed, as if you had struck him on the nose with a switch, stopped in his place and turned back, both out of shame and out of anger he turned towards home, let his horse into the stable and entered his room and closed the door on himself and pondered.

60. Gotinên pîrê anîn bîra xwe ku çima wilo jê re gotîye û qey ev qîza Mîrê Şêrwan çawa ye, çilo ye, qey gelekî xweşik û sipehî ye?
60. He brought the old woman's words to mind, why she had spoken to him like that, and what is this daughter of the Mîr of Şêrwan like, how is she, is she perhaps very beautiful and pretty?

61. Di odeya xwe de ma û roj bi roj pinisî û hêdî hêdî ji xwarinê qut bû û nexweş ket.
61. He remained in his room and pondered day by day, and slowly he was cut off from food and fell ill.

62. Dêya wî li ber digere nagere nema parîk nan dixwe, jinkê hewar kir Mîrê Botan û jê re got bigirî, û hêsir barandinê, kurê te ji dest xwe çûye hîn tu li odeya xwe ye li civatê ye hela were çavekî bike li ber bê çi dibêjê.
62. His mother pleaded and begged but he no longer eats a bite of bread, the woman cried out to the Mîr of Botan and said to him with weeping, and shedding tears at him, your son is out of his mind and you are still in your room in the assembly, come, take a look at him to see what he says.

63. Mîr: Ma çi bela wî ye jinik?
63. Mîr: What is the matter with him, woman?

64. Jinik: Bela wî ji wê roja cirîdê de ye roja Cejna Newrozê.
64. Woman: His trouble has been since that day of the jereed, the day of the Newroz Festival.

65. Mîr: Ma çima çi çêbûbû?
65. Mîr: Why, what had happened?

66. Çi qewimî bû?
66. What had occurred?

67. Jinik: Canîya kurê pîrê li pêşîya hespê wî xistibû, kurê te jî hêrs bûbû û dabû ser pişta kurê pîrê bi şivê.
67. Woman: The mare of the old woman's son had gotten in front of his horse, and your son had become angry and had gone after the old woman's son with a switch.

68. Mîr: Ê û çi gosert bû ku şivek lê daba û bêhna xwe derxista?
68. Mîr: And what disaster would it have been if he had struck him with a switch and let out his breath (vented his anger)?

69. Jinik: Goserta mezin ew bû, pîra dêya kurik çû pêşîyê û bi serê hespa kurê te girt û ew fedîkar kir.
69. Woman: The great disaster was that the old woman, the boy's mother, went in front and grabbed your son's horse's head and shamed him.

70. Mîr: Çima çi jê re got, û çi heqê wê heye serê hespa kurê min bigire?
70. Mîr: Why, what did she say to him, and what right does she have to grab my son's horse's head?

71. Jinik: Ya ji wilo mezintir û zehmettir jê re got.
71. Woman: She said to him something greater and harder than that.

72. Jinik: Pozê kurê te şikand û jê re got mala minê qîza Mîrê Şêrwan di ber de ye bê çi paye ye.
72. Woman: She broke your son's nose (pride) and said to him, 'My goodness, the daughter of the Mîr of Şêrwan is at stake, what arrogance is this'.

73. Mîr: Wey birawo, wey li min be kuro, çawa pîrek ji êlê kurtêlxuran mala me wilo ji kurê min re bibêje.
73. Mîr: Oh brother, woe is me, boy, how could an old woman from the tribe of scavengers of our house say such a thing to my son.

74. Hey hewar û sed hewar kuro kurtêlno, qirêjno, qerwaşno, herin herin pîrê û kurê wê werînin, çawa kurtêlxurê me li ber me rabin?
74. Hey alas and a hundred alas, boy, you scavengers, you filthy ones, you maids, go, go bring the old woman and her son, how can our scrap-eaters rise up against us?

75. Zaroyan bi çeplê pîrê û kurê pîrê girtin û anîn ber destê mîrî.
75. The children grabbed the arms of the old woman and the old woman's son and brought them before the mîr.

76. Mîr: Ha pîrê te çi gotîye ji Mamêd re?
76. Mîr: Ha, old woman, what have you said to Mamed?

77. Pîrê: Ezgorî min tişt negotîye, devê min şehetî û min peyvek got.
77. Old woman: May I be a sacrifice, I haven't said anything, my mouth slipped and I said a word.

78. Mîr: Devê te şehetî, devê te, ma çima tu nizanî ez û Mîrê Şerwan dijmjin in, tu pesnê qîza wî ji kurê min re didî?
78. Mîr: Your mouth slipped, your mouth, why, don't you know that I and the Mîr of Şêrwan are enemies, you praise his daughter to my son?

79. Pîrê: Ezgorî ma ez ji ku ve bi van tiştan dizanim.
79. Old woman: May I be a sacrifice, how am I to know about these things.

80. Mîr: Na na te ji qazî ve wilo jê re got ji bo kurik biqehere.
80. Mîr: No, no, you said it to him on purpose so that the boy would get angry.

81. Pîrê: Na ezgorî dilê min jê re dixwaze ku qîza Mîrê Şêrwan jê re be.
81. Old woman: No, may I be a sacrifice, my heart wishes for him that the daughter of the Mîr of Şêrwan be his.

82. Mîr: Kuro Lezgîno te çima li pêşîya wî xist?
82. Mîr: Boy, Lezgîn, why did you get in front of him?

83. Lezgîn: Mîrê min ezbenî min nizanîbû canîya min li pêşîya hespî wî xistîye, ma gunehê min çi ye?
83. Lezgîn: My mîr, my master, I did not know my mare had gotten in front of his horse, what is my sin?

84. Mîr: Gunehê te çi ye, çawa tê li pêşîya kurê min bixînî kuro, te serê canîya xwe ragirta.
84. Mîr: What is your sin, how could you get in front of my son, boy, you should have held back your mare's head.

85. Lezgîn: Ezbenî min bibexşîne ez bi van tiştan nizanim.
85. Lezgîn: My master, forgive me, I do not know about these things.

86. Pîrê: Ez bi gorîya serê te û serê kurê te bim, Lezgîn zaro ye nizane, ne ji qestî ve li pêşîya kurê te xistîye.
86. Old woman: May I be a sacrifice for your head and your son's head, Lezgîn is a child and does not know, he did not get in front of your son on purpose.

87. Cejna Newrozê bû û kêf û şahî bû xort li hespan siwar bûn û dan çargavê ji xwe re cirîd kirin, Lezgîn jî li canîka xwe siwar bû û da nav wan û wilo çêbû, ez gorî vê carê û ne tu cara din.
87. It was the Newroz Festival and it was fun and celebration, the youths mounted horses and set them to a gallop, playing jereed for themselves, Lezgîn too mounted his little mare and entered among them and it happened like that, may I be a sacrifice for this time and not any other time.

88. Û pîrê berê xwe da jina mîr û got: "Xatûna min ez cêrîya te me, ez ê her roj bêm karê te bikim, dewê te biklêm bi qerwaşî û vê carê hûn ê kurê min bibexşînin".
88. And the old woman turned to the mîr's wife and said: "My lady, I am your servant, I will come every day to do your chores, I will churn your ayran like a maid, and this time you will forgive my son".

89. Xatûn: De here here mala xwe tiştê te anîye serê me û te kurik dîn kirîye ma em ê çi bikin ji te û kurê te.
89. Lady: Come on, go, go to your house, the thing you brought upon us and you drove the boy crazy, what are we to do with you and your son.

90. Mîr û jina xwe hatin odeya kurê xwe û pê re axivîn.
90. The mîr and his wife came to their son's room and spoke with him.

235. For three months we went on the road, and afterwards three months on the return, in this way seven months were finished.

236. After we finished our Hajj and we did our preparations and chores, we turned toward home with the caravan.

330. She falls neither to the carpenter nor to the tailor, she falls to the religious man; by his hand and his prayers to God, soul came to her.

333. ## Sînem and Mela Itman

334. Di dema piştî fermandarîya Selahedîn de û vegera wî li bajarê Şamê gelek nûner û cîgir li herêm, îmaret, bajar, Şaristan û li hemû keleh û asêgehan man.
334. In the time after Saladin's command and his return to the city of Damascus, many representatives and deputies remained in the regions, emirates, cities, provinces, and in all the castles and strongholds.

335. After Saladin's passing to God's mercy.

336. Her emîrek û her fermandarek ma mîrê bi destê xwe, piştî çûna Al-Melak Al-Salih ber dilovanîya Xwedê Dewleta Eyûbîyan ji hev ket û perçe perç bû û Memalîkan zora wan bir û bi dû de Holako hat û herêm û kela û dizeyên mayîn di destê Eyûbîyan de giş herifandin wek Meyafarqîn û mîrê wê yê bi nav û deng.

Every emir and every commander remained a mîr by his own hand; after Al-Malik Al-Salih's passing to God's mercy, the Ayyubid State fell apart and was fragmented to pieces, and the Mamluks overpowered them, and afterwards Hulagu came, and all the remaining regions and castles and fortresses in the hands of the Ayyubids were ruined, like Meyafarqîn and its famous and renowned mîr.

337. One of Mîr Mamed's mother's begs was the commander of Egypt.

338. The city of Egypt is a very large and heavy city.

339. It is built over three hundred and sixty districts and neighborhoods.

340. In each neighborhood (quarter) there are thirty-three minarets.

341. Bajarê Misrê li ser ava mezin, çem û cobara ye. ye
341. The city of Egypt is on great water, rivers and streams.

342. Dibêjin di dema Mîr Mamed de miletê Misrê gelekî pêşketî bû bi bazirganî û çandinîyê, û dar û devîyên li dor rûbarê Nîlê çitar û mihela hebû, xerat, hesinkut, aş û qeraşa lê kar dikirin, û çi mizgefta hebû sofî û mela têde Quran dixwendin û feqe û suxte têde perwerde dikirin û ew fêrî ola misilmantîyê dikirin.
342. They say in the time of Mîr Mamed the nation of Egypt was very advanced in trade and agriculture, and the trees and bushes around the Nile river had camps and neighborhoods, carpenters, blacksmiths, millers and grinders worked there, and whatever mosque there was, sufis and melas read the Quran in it and students and disciples were educated in it and they taught them the religion of Islam.

343. Miletê Misrê bi kêf û şahî ye, çi kesê cot karîbe û çi kesê karmend be û çi kesê mela be û xwende û feqe û suxte be.
343. The nation of Egypt is with joy and celebration, whether one is a farmer and whether one is an official and whether one is a mela and student and pupil and disciple.

344. Dibêjin saleke xêr û bêr bû, miletê Misrê her yek bi karê xwe daketîye.
344. They say it was a year of goodness and blessing, the nation of Egypt each set to their own work.

345. Mela di mizgefta de bang kirin û gotin: "Bbûye dema kar û barê çûna Mala Xwedê, yê bixwaze û di dilê wî de ye çûna hecê bera karê xwe bike û navê xwe li cem başbazirganekî pêşkêşekê binivîse".
345. The melas in the mosques called out and said: "It has become the time for the chores of going to the House of God; whoever wants to and has going on the Hajj in his heart, let him make his preparations and write his name with a chief merchant for presentation".

346. Melakî qene bi hev re bimeşin û karibin xwe ji diz û çeta biparêzin.
346. A mela, so that they walk together and are able to protect themselves from thieves and bandits.

347. Mîr Mamed too wanted to go to the House of God; he had no children and could not bear to bring a second wife over his cousin (his wife).

348. 349. On the road the pilgrims were on foot and the merchants were mounted on horses and camels.

351. We will leave Mîr Mamed and the pilgrims and we will return to Egypt and go to Mela Itman and listen to his deeds and schemes.

352. Mela Itman was a covetous person, he was a scoundrel and untrustworthy, he was without conscience and malicious.

After the mîr turned his back to him and went on God's journey, Mela Itman fell into suspicions and circled the business of treachery and bad schemes around Sînem's palace, asking for her, wanting to speak with her; once, twice Sînem realized that the mela's intention is bad and his heart is impure and his eyes are the eyes of a rogue.

354. Sînem slowly withdrew herself, protected herself from him and no longer let the mela come close to her and her palace.
