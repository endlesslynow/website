Mustafa III (1757 – 1774) and the Ottoman Empire during his rule

Description

The provided text offers a comprehensive analysis of the Ottoman Empire under the reign of Sultan Mustafa III (1757-1774), characterizing it as a critical period defined by a ruler's theoretical desire for reform and his practical failure to execute it. The sources detail how Mustafa III, though intellectually prepared after two decades in confinement, lacked the experience necessary to address the Empire's systemic weaknesses, such as the rise of autonomous provincial notables (ayan) and the deterioration of the military. The article outlines key domestic events like the destabilizing 1766 Istanbul earthquake and the successful but temporary financial stability brought by Grand Vizier Koca Ragıp Pasha. Ultimately, the reign culminated in the catastrophic Russo-Turkish War (1768-1774), marked by the annihilation of the Ottoman military at the Battles of Kagul and Çeşme, which led to the humiliating Treaty of Küçük Kaynarca and formally initiated the "Eastern Question."
