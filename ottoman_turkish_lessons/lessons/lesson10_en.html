1. Interrogative Pronouns: كيم Kim? (Who?)

The Interrogative Pronouns are as follows. The interrogative sign می -mi is never used with them. Kim is applied to persons.

Examples:

Ottoman TurkishTransliterationTranslation
سن كيم سين؟sén kim sin?who are you?
كيم در او؟ كيم او؟ او كيم او؟kim dir o? kim o? o kim o?who is it?
كيمی كلدی كيمی كيتدیkimi géldi kimi gitdisome came others went
كيمم وار؟ كيمك وار؟ كيمی وار؟kimim var? kimiñ var? kimi var?whom do I have? who do you have? who does he have?
كيمسه سی يوقkimsési yoqhe has nobody
كيمككی؟kimiñki?whose?

2. Interrogative Pronouns: نه Né? (What?)

Applied to inanimate objects. Declined alone and with possessives.

Examples:

Ottoman TurkishTransliterationTranslation
نه در او؟ نه او؟né dir o? né o?What is it?
نه ايسترسكز؟né istérsiñiz?What do you want?
نملر؟ نه لری؟ném? nélér? néléri?what do I have? what things?
نه ديمك؟né démek?What does it mean?
نيچون = نه ايچون؟nî'chin? = né ichin?For what? Why?

3. Interrogative: هانكی Han'gî? (Which?)

Applied to persons and inanimate objects. قنغی is the old form, now obsolete.

Examples:

Ottoman TurkishTransliterationTranslation

هانكیمز؟han'gimiz?Which of us?
هانكیسندن؟han'gisindén?From which? / Of which?

هانكی آدم؟han'gî adém?Which man?

5. Interrogative: نصل Nasîl? & نيجه Nijé? (How? What kind?)

Nasîl means How? What sort of a thing? What kind?

Examples:

Ottoman TurkishTransliterationTranslation
نصل سكز؟na'sîl siñiz?How are you?
نصل بر آدم در؟na'sîl bir adém dir?What sort of a person is he?
بو آدم نيجه آدمدر؟bou adém nijé adémdir?What kind of a man is this?
نيجه دفعه لرnî'jé déf'alarHow many times!

6. Indefinite: كيمسه Kimsé (Anybody/Nobody)

Applied to persons only. Can be kimsé or kimésné.

8. Indefinite: هر Hér (Each, Every, -soever)

Hér is always an adjective and is used with all other indefinite pronouns.

Examples:

Ottoman TurkishTransliterationTranslation

هر نهhér néwhatsoever
هر هانكیhér ha'ngîwhichever
هر برhér bireach, every
هر يردهhér yérdéevery where
هر ايكیسیhér ikisiboth, each, either

9. Indefinite: بعض Ba'zî & بر آز Bir az (Some)

Ba'zî means a certain number. Bir az expresses a small quantity.

Examples:

15. Remarks مطالعات

a) The English pronoun "one" after an adjective is not expressed in Turkish.
b) "Somebody" is expressed by بری، بریسی biri, birisi.
c) "Each other" is expressed by بربری، يكدیگری birbiri, yékdigéri.

Examples:

Ottoman TurkishTransliterationTranslation
تازه صومون سنده می؟Tazé somoun séndé'mi?Do you have the fresh loaf?
بری سنی چاغیریيورBiri séni chaghîrîyor.Somebody is asking for you.
بربرينی سورلرBirbirini sévérlér.They love each other.
