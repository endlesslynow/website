Minorities in Kurdistan

Download Audio File

Description

These sources offer a comprehensive overview of minority communities across the geo-cultural region of Kurdistan, which spans southeastern Turkey, northern Iraq, northwestern Iran, and northern Syria. They highlight the diverse ethnic, religious, and linguistic groups present, including Assyrians, Yazidis, Turkmen, Armenians, and others. The texts emphasize how the political status and state policies of the dominant countries (Turkey, Iran, Iraq, Syria/AANES) profoundly impact minority rights, with different levels of recognition and protection across the four areas. Key challenges faced by these communities include displacement due to conflict, threats to cultural heritage, and struggles for linguistic and political rights.
