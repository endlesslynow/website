Significant Gaps in the Academic Record in Kurdish Oral History

Description

This text provides an overview of the current state of academic documentation of Kurdish oral history, highlighting significant gaps in research, particularly concerning everyday life, economic experiences, and the narratives of marginalized groups like LGBTQ+ individuals and religious minorities. It explains that geographical and linguistic imbalances in documentation are a result of the fragmented political landscape and the varying statuses of Kurdish dialects, with underrepresented languages like Zazaki and Gorani facing a risk of loss. The source emphasizes that these gaps are deeply rooted in the political realities of state suppression, linguicide, and statelessness, which have historically impeded research and cultural preservation. Finally, the text discusses the methodological, ethical, and archival challenges inherent in this field and proposes future research priorities, such as focusing on endangered languages, under-represented themes, and employing collaborative, interdisciplinary, and critical archival engagement methods to build a more complete record.
