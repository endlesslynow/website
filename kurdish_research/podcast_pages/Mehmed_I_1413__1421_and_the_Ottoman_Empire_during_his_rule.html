Mehmed I 1413 1421 and the Ottoman Empire during his rule

Description

The provided text details the restoration of the Ottoman Empire by Sultan Mehmed I following the devastating Battle of Ankara in 1402 and the subsequent Ottoman Interregnum. It describes how Mehmed I, often called the "second founder," reunified the fractured empire through a combination of strategic military victories and astute diplomacy against his brothers. The document also highlights his efforts to re-establish Ottoman legitimacy through a cultivated persona, symbolic acts like minting coins, and significant architectural patronage such as the Green Complex in Bursa. Furthermore, it examines his pragmatic approach to governance and economic recovery, focusing on consolidating central authority, restoring the timar system, professionalizing the Janissaries, and addressing challenges like the Sheikh Bedreddin revolt. Finally, the text outlines his cautious foreign policy, which prioritized internal stability and securing borders, even enduring a naval defeat against Venice at Gallipoli to avoid overextension.
