The mid-17th-century RozhîkîBitlis revolt led by Abdal Khan Emir of Bitlis

Description

This source examines the 1655 revolt of Abdal Khan, the semi-autonomous Kurdish Emir of Bitlis, against the centralizing Ottoman Empire. It highlights how the Treaty of Zuhab in 1639 rendered the strategic buffer role of Kurdish emirates obsolete, leading the Ottomans to assert direct control. The text portrays Abdal Khan as a polymath and powerful ruler who cultivated a Persianate court culture and maintained a formidable military, directly challenging Ottoman authority. The revolt culminated in the sacking of Bitlis and Abdal Khan's eventual execution in Istanbul, marking a turning point in Ottoman-Kurdish relations towards direct imperial rule. The source relies heavily on the biased but detailed account of Evliya Çelebi, an Ottoman traveler.
