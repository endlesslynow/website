Description

Established in 1983, the Kurdish Institute of Paris (KIP) serves as a vital center for Kurdish culture, language, and history, particularly for the diaspora. Responding to severe repression, it aimed to provide a unified voice and hope while operating as an independent, non-political, and secular entity. The KIP engages in extensive linguistic work, notably standardizing the Kurmanji dialect, hosts the largest collection of Kurdish materials in the West in its library, and runs academic support programs, including for women scholars. Despite facing financial challenges, it actively promotes Kurdish cultural heritage through various events and has historically played a significant role in international advocacy for Kurdish rights. Ultimately, the KIP remains a crucial institution dedicated to preserving identity and fostering an informed diaspora.
