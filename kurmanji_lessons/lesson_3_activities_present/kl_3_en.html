2. Present Tense

The present tense is formed by adding the prefix "di-" and the suffix "-im, -î, -e, -in" to the verb root:

Pronoun Present tense prefix Root Personal suffix Sentence
Ez di ç im Ez diçim.
Tu di ç î Tu diçî.
Ew di ç e Ew diçe.
Em di ç in Em diçin.
Hûn di ç in Hûn diçin.
Ew di ç in Ew diçin.

Note: The present tense in Kurdish typically follows this pattern: pronoun + di- + verb root + personal ending.

3. Negative Present Tense

To make a present tense verb negative, we replace the prefix "di-" with "na-":

Verb English Present form Negative form
nivîsîn to write dinivîsim nanivîsim
kenîn to laugh dikenim nakenim
meşîn to walk dimeşim nameşim
fikirîn to think difikirîm nafikirîm
pirsîn to ask dipirsim napirsim

Note: The negative form follows the pattern: Pronoun + na- + verb root + personal ending

4. Prepositions

Prepositions are important words that show relationships between different elements in a sentence. They help indicate location, direction, time, and other relationships.

Common Prepositions

Li - Used to indicate location ("at", "in", "on")
Ji - Used to express source or origin ("from", "of")
Di...de - Used to express "inside" or "within" something

1. Li (at, in, on)

"Li" is used to indicate location or position. It translates to "at," "in," or "on" depending on the context.

Example Translation Explanation
Ez li Amedê dijîm. I live in Amed. Indicates location within a city
Ew li malê ye. He/She is at home. Indicates presence at a location
Pirtûk li ser maseyê ye. The book is on the table. Combined with "ser" (top) to indicate position

2. Ji (from, of)

"Ji" is used to express origin, source, or starting point. It can also be used to express preference.

Example Translation Explanation
Ez ji Amedê têm. I come from Amed. Indicates origin or source
Ew ji saet şeşan şûnda kardikin. They work from six o'clock. Indicates starting time
Ez ji muzîkê hez dikim. I like music. (lit: I from music like do) Used with "hez kirin" to express liking something

3. Di...de (in, within, inside)

"Di...de" is a circumposition (a preposition that surrounds the word) used to express being inside or within something.

Example Translation Explanation
Pirtûk di çanteyê de ye. The book is in the bag. Indicates location inside something
Em di havînê de diçin deryayê. We go to the sea in summer. Indicates time period
Ez di saet şeşan de radibim. I get up at six o'clock. Indicates specific point in time

5. Dialogue

Read this simple dialogue that uses vocabulary and grammar from this lesson:

Azad: Rojbaş! Tu çawa yî?
Hello! How are you?
Rojin: Ez baş im, spas. Tu çi dikî?
I'm good, thanks. What are you doing?
Azad: Ez pirtûkê dixwînim. Tu li malê çi dikî?
I'm reading a book. What do you do at home?
Rojin: Ez li malê wêneyan xêz dikim û televizyonê temaşe dikim.
I draw pictures at home and watch television.
Azad: Di derveyî malê de, tu çi dikî?
Outside of home, what do you do?
Rojin: Ez di parkê de digerim û bi hevalên xwe re dilîzim.
I walk around in the park and play with my friends.
Azad: Tu taştê dixwî?
Do you eat breakfast?
Rojin: Erê, ez her roj taştê dixwim. Lê ez şîr navexwim.
Yes, I eat breakfast every day. But I don't drink milk.
Azad: Ez ji çayê hez dikim. Em bi hev re diaxivin?
I like tea. Shall we talk together?
Rojin: Baş e! Ez ji axaftinê hez dikim.
Good! I like talking.
