Mustafa II (1695 – 1703) and the Ottoman Empire during his rule

Description

The provided source is an extensive historical report analyzing the pivotal reign of Sultan Mustafa II (1695-1703), arguing that this period was the crucible for the Ottoman Empire's transition from a conquering power to a declining state. The report details the Sultan's personal ambition to revive the warrior-sultan role, which was initially met with success but led to the catastrophic defeat at the Battle of Zenta in 1697. This military disaster resulted in the Sultan's psychological withdrawal and the subsequent rise of corrupt, unaccountable rule, which directly caused the Edirne Incident of 1703 and his deposition. Crucially, the source identifies the Treaty of Karlowitz (1699), which formalized massive territorial losses, as the definitive external marker of the empire's irreversible geopolitical realignment and the beginning of the "Eastern Question."
