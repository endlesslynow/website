The Kurdish Epic of Dimdim Castle

Download Audio File

Description

This report provides a detailed analysis of the Kurdish Epic of Dimdim Castle, known as Beytî Dimdim, a significant narrative in Kurdish culture second only to Mem û Zîn. It explores the epic's origins in the historical Battle of Dimdim (1609-1610) between Kurdish forces led by Emîr Xan Lepzêrîn and the Safavid Empire under Shah Abbas I. The analysis covers the epic's narrative structure, key characters, literary themes (including heroism, sacrifice, and resistance), and its enduring cultural and political importance as a symbol of Kurdish identity and aspiration for freedom. The text highlights how the epic serves as a counter-narrative to official historical accounts and details its transmission through oral tradition and literary works, along with significant scholarly efforts to preserve and interpret it.
