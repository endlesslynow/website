Murad I 1362 1389 and the Ottoman Empire during his rule

Description

The provided source offers a comprehensive analysis of Sultan Murad I's pivotal reign, highlighting his transformation of the Ottoman entity from a frontier principality into an organized empire. It examines Murad's complex character as both a pious warrior and a ruthless statesman, emphasizing his strategic use of dynastic marriages and brutal consolidation of power, including the execution of his rebellious brothers and son. The text extensively details Murad's military expansions into both the Balkans and Anatolia, noting the capture of Adrianople as a strategic turning point and the costly Ottoman victory at the Battle of Kosovo, where he was killed. Crucially, the source outlines the foundational institutions he established, such as the Janissary corps and the devşirme system for military recruitment, the formalization of the Divan and Beylerbeyi offices for centralized administration, and the implementation of the timar system for land management and military financing. It concludes by discussing his pragmatic governance of diverse populations, particularly the application of the dhimmi system for non-Muslims, underscoring his lasting legacy as the true architect of the classical Ottoman Empire.
