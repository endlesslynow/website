Description

The provided text explores the multifaceted Kurdish mythology, encompassing its oral traditions, pantheon of deities and mythical creatures, and major narrative cycles such as the Kawa and Zahhak epic and Mem û Zîn. It examines the historical and cultural influences that have shaped this mythology, including ancient Iranian and Mesopotamian roots, as well as the unique syncretism found in Yazidism. The sources also discuss the ongoing relevance of folklore to Kurdish identity, cultural practices like Newroz, and its role in cultural preservation and national consciousness. Furthermore, the text outlines the transmission and documentation of Kurdish mythology, highlighting the shift from oral tradition to written archives and the significant scholarly engagement with this rich heritage.
