Mahmud I (1730 – 1754) and the Ottoman Empire during his rule

Download Audio File

Description

The provided text offers a comprehensive analysis of the 24-year reign of Sultan Mahmud I (1730–1754), framing it as a crucial period that refutes the traditional Ottoman Decline Thesis. The sources characterize the Sultan as an intellectual, adaptive ruler whose personality was shaped by 27 years of confinement, enabling him to skillfully suppress the Patrona Halil Rebellion and govern through a stable inner circle. Militarily, his era was one of profound resilience, marked by successful two-front wars that culminated in the decisive Treaty of Belgrade (1739) against Austria and Russia, which re-secured crucial Balkan territories. Furthermore, the reign initiated early modernity through targeted military reforms, like the Humbaracı corps, and a cultural renaissance exemplified by the development of the opulent Ottoman Baroque architectural style.
