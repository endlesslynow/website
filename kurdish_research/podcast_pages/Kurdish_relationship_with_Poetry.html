Kurdish relationship with Poetry

Description

This source provides an in-depth exploration of Kurdish poetry, highlighting its profound importance as a cultural cornerstone and a repository of history and identity for a stateless people. It traces the tradition from ancient oral forms like the dengbêj through classical masters such as Ahmad Khani to modern figures like Sherko Bekas, noting poetry's role as a vehicle for resistance and social critique. The text also discusses the distinct linguistic landscape of Kurdish dialects and the challenges and significance of translation and preservation, ultimately emphasizing poetry's enduring power as a testament to Kurdish resilience.
