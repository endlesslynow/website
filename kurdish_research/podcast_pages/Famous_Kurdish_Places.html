Description

This report examines Kurdistan, a geo-cultural region spanning parts of Western Asia, highlighting its deep historical roots and enduring Kurdish identity. It surveys natural landscapes like the Zagros and Taurus mountains and major rivers, emphasizing their symbolic importance and role in shaping culture. The text explores numerous ancient and archaeological sites, including early settlements and classical-era ruins, demonstrating the region's long history of human habitation. It also details the significance of historic cities, such as Erbil and Diyarbakır, as centers of Kurdish life, and discusses cultural and religious sanctuaries, particularly the holy site of Lalish for the Yezidi faith. Finally, the report touches upon modern cultural institutions and artistic expressions that work to preserve and promote Kurdish heritage.
