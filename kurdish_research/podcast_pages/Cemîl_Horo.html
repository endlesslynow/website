Cemîl Horo

Description

The provided source details the life and legacy of Cemîl Horo (1934-1989), an influential Kurdish Dengbêj (singing storyteller) from Afrin, Syria. It highlights his role in preserving Kurdish oral traditions through his powerful voice and diverse repertoire, which included ancient epics, personal laments, and political anthems. The text recounts his struggles against state persecution, torture, and forced exile, as well as the profound personal tragedy of his son's murder. Despite these hardships, Horo's artistic dedication and commitment to Kurdish identity endured, influencing his sons, who continue his musical legacy. His enduring impact is further evidenced by the Cemîl Horo Center for Culture and Art, a cultural institution dedicated to safeguarding Kurdish heritage, demonstrating how his legacy transitioned from individual expression to a broader societal preservation effort.
