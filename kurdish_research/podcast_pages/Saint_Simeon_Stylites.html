Description

The provided sources offer a comprehensive overview of St. Simeon Stylites the Elder, a prominent 5th-century Syrian ascetic. They explore his extraordinary life atop a pillar, detailing his radical ascetic practices and their motivations, such as seeking solitude and expressing profound piety. The texts further illuminate the historical and geographical context of his influence, including the development of the pilgrimage complex at Qal'at Sim'an. Crucially, the sources emphasize Simeon's role as a significant political and theological figure within the Eastern Roman Empire, particularly his unwavering support for the Chalcedonian Definition amidst the Christological controversies that fractured the Christian church.
