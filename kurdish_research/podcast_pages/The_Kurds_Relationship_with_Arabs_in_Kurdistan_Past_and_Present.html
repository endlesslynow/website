The Kurds Relationship with Arabs in Kurdistan Past and Present

Description

These sources offer a detailed historical and contemporary analysis of the complex relationship between Kurds and Arabs in the geo-cultural region of Kurdistan, which is divided across several modern states including Iraq and Syria. They trace this dynamic from early Islamic conquests and the rise of Kurdish emirates to the traumatic post-Ottoman partition, the impact of Arab nationalism leading to policies of assimilation and brutal repression like the Anfal campaign in Iraq, and the challenges and opportunities for coexistence and autonomy in the Kurdistan Regional Government (KRG) in Iraq and the Autonomous Administration of North and East Syria (AANES/Rojava). The texts highlight persistent disputes over sovereignty, resources, and disputed territories in Iraq and explore the AANES's efforts towards multi-ethnic governance alongside local tensions, grievances, and the significant influence of external actors on Kurd-Arab dynamics, particularly in Syria following the civil war and the fall of the Assad regime.
