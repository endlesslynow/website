Mehmed VI 1918 1922 and the Ottoman Empire during his rule

Description

The provided text offers an extensive historical analysis of the final, turbulent years of the Ottoman Empire, focusing on the reign of the last Sultan, Mehmed VI Vahideddin (1918–1922). The document is structured in two parts: first, a detailed portrait of the Sultan, examining his psychology, failed Anglophile political strategy, and his fratricidal conflict with Mustafa Kemal's Turkish National Movement, which included issuing religious decrees and authorizing an anti-nationalist army. The second section shifts focus to the Empire's social and economic collapse under Allied occupation, describing the legal chaos of the "dual power" between Istanbul and Ankara, devastating hyperinflation that crushed the populace, and the profound societal strains caused by the influx of massive refugee waves and the deadly Spanish Flu pandemic. Ultimately, the sources detail the Sultan's humiliation—culminating in his flight aboard a British battleship and his eventual death in poverty—as the imperial cosmopolitan state irrevocably gave way to the Turkish nation-state.
