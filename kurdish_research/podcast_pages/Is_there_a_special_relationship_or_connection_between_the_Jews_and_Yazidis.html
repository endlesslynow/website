Description

The provided texts offer an extensive examination of the Yezidi faith, drawing from both historical research and contemporary fieldwork. They explore the Yezidis' complex religious identity, highlighting how their oral traditions contain Late Antique motifs from various sources like Gnosticism, Manichaeism, Judaism, and Syriac Christianity, rather than being solely derived from a single ancient tradition. The texts also address the Yezidis' socio-political challenges as a minority group in the Middle East, particularly in Syria, discussing their historical persecution, efforts to preserve their distinct culture and language, and recent developments in self-governance and education. Finally, the sources shed light on Yezidi religious concepts, such as the role of the Peacock Angel, the creation myths of Adam and Seth, and the significance of the khirqe (a sacred garment), acknowledging the ongoing evolution and interpretation of their beliefs.
