Armenian Accounts of Afrin

Download Audio File

Description

This source examines the historical presence of Armenians in Syria's Afrin district before 2010, emphasizing that their communities were largely formed by survivors of the Armenian Genocide (1915-1923) seeking refuge. It details how, despite a tragic origin, Armenians in Afrin generally experienced peaceful coexistence with the Kurdish majority, often due to shared marginalization by the Syrian state. The text highlights the socio-cultural life of these scattered communities, noting their efforts to preserve identity through institutions like churches and schools, and describes their integration into the region's agrarian economy. Ultimately, the source concludes that the Armenian presence in Afrin was gradually diminishing even before the Syrian Civil War, primarily due to emigration and assimilation rather than local persecution.
