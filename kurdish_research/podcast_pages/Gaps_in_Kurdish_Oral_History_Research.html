Gaps in Kurdish Oral History Research

Download Audio File

Description

The sources collectively explore the significant gaps in the academic study of Kurdish oral history, despite its crucial role as a repository of cultural memory and identity for a stateless nation. They highlight that while research has focused on figures like dengbêjs and the impact of orality on modern literature, many areas remain underexplored, including the oral histories of women beyond narratives of conflict, everyday life, and the traditions of diverse religious and ethnic subgroups. The authors attribute these gaps to political repression, challenges in archival accessibility and organization, methodological difficulties in researching conflict zones, and institutional and funding disparities within Kurdish studies, emphasizing the need for collaborative and ethical approaches to achieve a more comprehensive understanding.
