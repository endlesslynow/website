Bayezid I 1389 1402

Description

The provided text offers an extensive overview of Sultan Bayezid I's reign (1389-1402), focusing on his ambitious attempts to transform the decentralized Ottoman ghazi state into a centralized, bureaucratic empire. It details his early life, military prowess that earned him the moniker "Thunderbolt," and his ruthless consolidation of power, notably the fratricide of his brother, Yakub Çelebi, to secure his throne. The document further describes his dual-front expansionist policies in both the Balkans, culminating in the decisive victory at Nicopolis in 1396, and his aggressive campaigns to unify Anatolia, which led to a confrontation with Timur. Finally, it recounts Bayezid's catastrophic defeat and capture at the Battle of Ankara in 1402, discussing the controversial narratives surrounding his captivity and the subsequent eleven-year Ottoman Interregnum, ultimately framing his reign as a crucial, albeit failed, precursor to the empire's later, more stable "second founding."
