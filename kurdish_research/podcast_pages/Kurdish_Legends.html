Description

These sources provide a comprehensive overview of Kurdish folklore, highlighting its deep connection to Kurdish identity and resistance. They emphasize the crucial role of dengbêj, traditional bards who preserved oral traditions like the liberation myth of Kawa the Blacksmith and the tragic epic of Mem û Zîn, which have evolved into powerful symbols of national struggle and unity. The sources also examine the complex figure of Shahmaran, the serpent queen representing wisdom and healing, and touch upon other significant narratives such as the historical epic of Dimdim and various origin myths, demonstrating the rich and dynamic nature of Kurdish cultural heritage in both oral and written forms, often within a contested political landscape.
