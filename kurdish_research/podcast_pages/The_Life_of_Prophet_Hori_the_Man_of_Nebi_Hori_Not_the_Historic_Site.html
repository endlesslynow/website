The Life of Prophet Hori the Man of Nebi Hori Not the Historic Site

Description

The provided text explores the complex identity of "Prophet Hori" at the sacred site of Nebi Hori in northern Syria, asserting that he is not a single historical individual but a layered embodiment of the site's rich history. Beginning as a Roman military commander's tomb, the location later transformed into a Christian "City of Saints," housing relics and associated with an apostle. During the Mamluk Sultanate, it was re-consecrated as an Islamic shrine for Nebi Hori, identified with the biblical figure Uriah the Hittite, even as theological interpretations of Uriah's story varied. Today, Hori is revered as a local Sufi saint and, more recently, linked to ancient Hurrian ancestry, reflecting modern Kurdish identity formation. The recent controversial reconstruction by Turkish authorities highlights the ongoing struggle over the site's cultural and historical narrative, demonstrating how Prophet Hori's identity remains a dynamic and contested symbol of the region's diverse heritage.
