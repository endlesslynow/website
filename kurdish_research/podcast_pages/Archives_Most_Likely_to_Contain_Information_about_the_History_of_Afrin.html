Archives Most Likely to Contain Information about the History of Afrin

Download Audio File

Description

This report provides a guide to archival repositories essential for researching the history of the Afrin region in Syria. It highlights how Afrin's history, from ancient times through the Ottoman Empire, French Mandate, and modern Syrian state, is documented in sources dispersed across various countries. Key institutions discussed include archives in Turkey, France, the UK, Syria, and Germany, reflecting the diverse administrations that governed or had interests in the area. The text also underscores the growing importance of Kurdish-focused archives and digital projects, along with oral histories and local collections, for capturing often marginalized perspectives. Challenges like linguistic barriers and accessibility are noted, emphasizing the need for a multi-archival approach to fully understand Afrin's complex past.
